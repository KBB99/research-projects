\begin{abstract}
% ==================================================

Attention mechanisms are typically introduced as architectural primitives motivated by empirical performance in sequence modeling. In this essay, we present an alternative perspective: attention arises as a \emph{structurally necessary interaction} in a broad class of entropy-regulated relational field theories. Working within the Relativistic Scalar--Vector--Plenum (RSVP) framework, we formulate a set of analytic, symmetry, and bottleneck constraints on effective dynamics and show that, in the permutation-equivariant and entropy-implicit regime, the lowest-order nontrivial interaction permitted by these constraints coincides with the self-attention structure.

This formulation reframes attention not as an ad hoc mechanism, but as the minimal quartic interaction allowed by relational invariance and feature-space symmetry breaking, analogous to renormalizable interactions in effective field theory. By explicitly introducing an entropy field, we further demonstrate how this minimal interaction deforms, sparsifies, or collapses outside its regime of validity, yielding principled predictions for architectural phase transitions. The resulting view situates attention as a special case of a more general entropic field dynamics governing structured computation.

\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ==================================================

The Transformer architecture and its variants have reshaped contemporary approaches to sequence modeling, largely through the introduction of self-attention as a dominant interaction mechanism. Despite extensive empirical validation, the conceptual status of attention remains unsettled: it is often treated either as an inspired engineering construct or as a generic mechanism for context aggregation.

A growing body of theoretical work suggests a stronger claim: that attention is not merely effective, but \emph{inevitable} under suitable structural assumptions. Independently, the RSVP framework has argued that cognition and computation are governed by entropy-regulated field dynamics subject to relational invariance, symmetry breaking, and bottlenecked interaction channels. These two lines of thought converge on a common question: under what conditions does attention emerge as the minimal interaction compatible with relational structure?

The aim of this essay is to make that convergence explicit. Rather than deriving an architecture, we classify admissible interactions under RSVP structural postulates and show that attention appears as the unique lowest-order interacting term in a particular symmetry phase. This reframing places attention on the same conceptual footing as renormalizable interactions in physics: selected not by design preference, but by structural necessity.

\section{RSVP Configuration Space}
% ==================================================

We consider a system defined over a discrete set of $N$ tokens, each carrying a $C$-dimensional feature vector. The RSVP framework models such a system as a collection of interacting fields rather than as a static representation.

\begin{definition}[RSVP Fields]
An RSVP configuration consists of:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a scalar field $\Phi \in \mathbb{R}^{N\times C}$ encoding content, density, or semantic mass,
  \item a vector field $\mathbf v \in \mathbb{R}^{N\times C}$ encoding transport, coupling, or directed flow,
  \item an entropy field $S \in \mathbb{R}^{N}$ encoding local uncertainty or disorder.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

For analytical convenience, the scalar field may be complexified as
\[
X = \Phi_1 + i \Phi_2 \in \mathbb{C}^{N\times C}.
\]
This representation allows compact expression of symmetry constraints and aligns with complex-valued formulations of effective dynamics. Importantly, no physical interpretation is attached to complexification; it is a mathematical convenience reflecting paired degrees of freedom.

\section{Structural Optimality Axioms}
% ==================================================

We now articulate the structural assumptions that define admissible effective dynamics. These axioms are not specific to neural architectures; they reflect generic constraints on relational, entropy-regulated systems.

\begin{axiom}[Analytic Effective Description]
The dynamics of the system are generated by an effective free energy $\mathcal F(X,S)$ that is analytic in $X$ and $X^\*$ and smooth in $S$. Higher-order interaction terms are progressively suppressed by scale separation or entropy.
\end{axiom}

Analyticity encodes the effective field theory assumption that only a finite number of low-order interactions are relevant at macroscopic scales.

\begin{axiom}[Relational Token Invariance]
For any token isometry $P \in U(N)$,
\[
\mathcal F(PX,PS)=\mathcal F(X,S).
\]
\end{axiom}

This axiom asserts that dynamics depend only on relational structure between tokens, not on their absolute indices. It generalizes permutation invariance to a coordinate-free statement.

\begin{axiom}[Feature Isotropy with Symmetry Breaking]
There exist learnable operators $W_1,\dots,W_n \in \mathbb{C}^{C\times C}$ such that for any feature rotation
\[
R \in \mathrm{Cent}(\{W_i\}) \subset U(C),
\quad
\mathcal F(XR^\*,S)=\mathcal F(X,S).
\]
\end{axiom}

Feature space is assumed isotropic except along directions explicitly selected by interaction operators. Semantic structure arises through symmetry breaking, not through privileged coordinates.

\begin{axiom}[Bottlenecked Mediation]
Each interaction operator $W_i$ has rank bounded by $C_A \ll C$, enforcing low-dimensional mediation of interactions.
\end{axiom}

This axiom encodes the empirical and theoretical necessity of bottlenecks, limiting entropy flow and preventing full thermalization.

\section{Canonical Form of the Free Energy}
% ==================================================

The above axioms impose strong restrictions on the functional form of $\mathcal F$.

\begin{lemma}[Canonical Invariant Form]
Under the preceding axioms, the free energy depends on $X$ only through the spectral invariants of matrices $X W_i X^\*$:
\[
\mathcal F(X,S)=f\big(XW_1X^\*,\dots,XW_nX^\*;S\big),
\]
where $f$ is an analytic spectral function.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Token invariance restricts dependence to $U(N)$-invariant quantities, which are functions of Gram-type matrices. Feature symmetry further constrains these to combinations involving the $W_i$. Analyticity ensures that no additional nonlocal structure appears.
\end{proof}

This result expresses a fundamental principle: relational dynamics factor through relational observables.

% ==================================================
\section{Minimal Interaction and Attention}
% ==================================================

Expanding $f$ as a Taylor series around the origin yields a hierarchy of admissible interaction terms.

\begin{lemma}[Lowest-Order Interactions]
The lowest-order nontrivial truncation consists of:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a quadratic term in $X$, yielding linear mixing,
  \item a quartic term in $X$, yielding bilinear--bilinear coupling.
\end{itemize}
Cubic terms are forbidden by the symmetry constraints.
\end{lemma}

The quadratic term produces a purely linear flow equivalent to a feed-forward mixing. The quartic term takes the general form
\[
\sum_{i,j} \mathrm{Tr}\!\left(X W_i X^\* X W_j X^\*\right),
\]
whose gradient generates interaction terms of the form
\[
\dot X \sim X(X^\*X).
\]

\begin{theorem}[Minimal Relational Interaction]
In the permutation-equivariant, entropy-implicit regime, the quartic interaction above is the unique minimal interacting term permitted by the structural axioms. Its induced dynamics are equivalent, up to reparameterization, to self-attention.
\end{theorem}

Thus, attention is not selected by heuristic design but by structural inevitability, analogous to $\phi^4$ interactions in scalar field theory.

% ==================================================
\section{Entropy-Explicit Extensions and Phase Transitions}
% ==================================================

RSVP extends purely structural analyses by promoting entropy to an explicit dynamical field.

Define
\[
\mathcal F(X,S)=\mathcal U(X)-\tau\mathcal H(S)+\mathcal C(X,S),
\]
where $\mathcal U$ is the analytic structural potential, $\mathcal H$ an entropy functional, and $\mathcal C$ encodes constraints.

\begin{corollary}[Entropy Saturation]
When entropy gradients vanish, the quartic interaction becomes effectively irrelevant and the system reduces to isotropic linear mixing.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Entropy-Driven Deformation]
Strong entropy gradients or constraints break full token symmetry, forcing the minimal interaction to deform into sparse, localized, or directed variants.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Bottleneck Capacity]
If entropy transport demand exceeds bottleneck capacity, the system must either increase mediator rank, fragment interactions, or compress semantic structure.
\end{corollary}

These results characterize attention as a phase-dependent interaction, not a universal primitive.

% ==================================================
\section{Conclusion}
% ==================================================

We have shown that attention emerges as the minimal relational interaction in an entropy-regulated field theory under analyticity, relational invariance, and symmetry-breaking constraints. In this regime, attention is unavoidable; outside it, attention must deform or give way to alternative structures.

This perspective reframes architectural design as phase selection within a broader dynamical theory and situates attention within a general class of entropy-mediated interactions governing structured computation.

\section{Gauge Symmetry and Permutation Equivariance}
% ==================================================

This appendix clarifies the role of gauge symmetry and permutation equivariance in the RSVP formulation and explains how conventional permutation-equivariant architectures arise as a particular gauge-fixed phase of a more general relational symmetry.

% --------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Relational Symmetry as Gauge Redundancy}
% --------------------------------------------------

In the RSVP framework, token labels carry no intrinsic meaning. Physical or semantic content is encoded entirely in relational structure between tokens. This principle is formalized by invariance under the action of a symmetry group $G$ acting on token indices.

When $G = U(N)$ or its discrete subgroup $S_N$, this symmetry is naturally interpreted as a \emph{gauge redundancy}: multiple field configurations related by the action of $G$ represent the same physical or semantic state.

Accordingly, the RSVP configuration space is not a naive vector space of fields but a quotient object, represented in the main text as a derived stack
\[
\mathcal M_{\mathrm{RSVP}} = [\mathbb{C}^{N\times C} / G].
\]
Gauge-equivalent configurations correspond to different coordinate descriptions of the same relational state.

% --------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Permutation Equivariance as a Gauge Choice}
% --------------------------------------------------

Permutation-equivariant architectures impose the condition that all operations commute with the action of the permutation group $S_N$. In RSVP terms, this corresponds to working in a gauge where relational symmetry is preserved explicitly at every step of the dynamics.

From the gauge-theoretic perspective, permutation equivariance is not a fundamental requirement but a \emph{choice of gauge} that keeps the redundancy manifest. Other gauges are possible in which relational symmetry is partially fixed or spontaneously broken, yielding architectures with positional bias, locality, or causal structure.

Thus, permutation equivariance should be understood as a symmetry phase rather than as an absolute constraint.

% --------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Gauge-Invariant Observables}
% --------------------------------------------------

Physical or semantic observables in RSVP must be gauge invariant. Under relational symmetry, admissible observables are functions of invariant combinations such as:
\[
X^\*X, \quad XW_iX^\*, \quad \text{or spectral data thereof}.
\]

These quantities are invariant under the action of $G$ and therefore descend to well-defined functions on the quotient stack. The restriction to such observables explains why attention kernels are necessarily relational and why absolute token indices never appear in the effective dynamics.

% --------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Gauge Symmetry and Attention}
% --------------------------------------------------

The attention interaction derived in the main text is gauge invariant by construction. The quartic interaction
\[
\mathrm{Tr}(XW_iX^\*XW_jX^\*)
\]
depends only on relational combinations of fields and therefore respects the full relational gauge symmetry.

From the gauge-theoretic viewpoint, attention is the minimal interaction that:
\begin{itemize}
  \item couples tokens through gauge-invariant observables,
  \item remains nontrivial after quotienting by $G$,
  \item preserves locality in feature space while remaining global in token space.
\end{itemize}

No lower-order interaction satisfies these conditions.

% --------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Gauge Fixing, Positional Structure, and Symmetry Breaking}
% --------------------------------------------------

Introducing positional encodings, causal masks, or locality constraints corresponds to partial gauge fixing or spontaneous breaking of relational symmetry. In such cases:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the full permutation group is reduced to a subgroup,
  \item additional invariant structures become admissible,
