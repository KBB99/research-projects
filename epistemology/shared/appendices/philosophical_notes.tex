\section{Philosophical Notes}

This appendix situates the formal results within broader philosophical traditions.

\subsection{Action Without Intention}

Agency here does not presuppose intention or consciousness. Structural dependence of action on constraints suffices.

\subsection{Refusal and Autonomy}

Refusal is autonomy in its minimal form: the capacity to suspend execution without justification.

This aligns with:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Melville's \emph{Bartleby} (non-instrumental refusal),
  \item Agamben's inoperativity,
  \item Indigenous practices of strategic illegibility.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Against Didactic Reduction}

Didactic interfaces collapse judgment into procedure. This mirrors:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Weberian rationalization,
  \item Arendt's banality of evil,
  \item Braverman's deskilling.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Semantic Impedance}

Dense, redundant, or historically layered exposition is not a fallacy when it preserves retrocomputability. It functions as semantic impedance under adversarial compression.

\subsection{Limits of Formalization}

This appendix itself is an abstraction. It preserves invariants while excluding lived judgment. That exclusion is acknowledged, not denied.
