How can understanding itself—an act of coherence across time, sense,
and scale—be described by a single physical and mathematical law?
Current models divide explanation among thermodynamics, information theory, and compu-
tation, yet none specify why systems capable of prediction and meaning stabilize as they do.
This work proposes that cognition, physics, and inference are all manifestations of a single
principle: **autogeneration**—the self-maintenance of informational curvature within a
cohesive medium.
Beginning from the question of how systems learn without external supervision, **Part I**
derives a thermodynamic and geometric account of cognition as a non-Markovian flow whose
equilibrium defines comprehension. **Part II** reformulates this process categorically,
showing that learning corresponds to adjoint functorial transformations and renormalization
across interpretive scales. **Part III** integrates quantum, differential, and topological
layers, culminating in a **cohesive ∞-topos of cognition** governed by an idempotent
autogenerative functor A.
The resulting **Unified Conjecture** claims that every process of comprehension—neural,
symbolic, or cosmological—is an instance of this curvature-preserving closure: **to know is
to remain self-consistent under transformation**.
Understanding is the unique fixed point of reality.

"To exist is to learn. This work aims to show that learning, at every level of
the universe, is the process by which curvature maintains itself— the
autogenerative equilibrium we call understanding."

1.2
Introduction: The Autogenerative Hypothesis
Language, cognition, and natural computation exhibit an intrinsic property of autogeneration:
each moment of activity encodes the conditions for its own continuation. The concept, articu-
lated by Barenholtz (2025), redefines autoregression as more than a statistical convenience;
it is the very mode of natural self-propagation. In contrast to Markovian processes, where
the next state depends only on the current one, autogenerative systems draw upon the entire
trajectory of prior states. They are inherently non-Markovian, operating on the accumulated
curvature of experience rather than on instantaneous state vectors.
This view renders the corpus of language not merely as data but as a field of continuation
rules—a statistical manifold whose internal topology determines its own dynamics. In such a
manifold, the "pregnant present" is a boundary condition that enfolds its history and projects
its own future trajectory. Autogeneration therefore serves as the bridge between memory,
inference, and meaning. The goal of this section is to formalize that bridge using information
geometry, category theory, and dynamical-systems analysis.
