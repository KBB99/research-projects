Curvatures of Passage

paths through controlled variation of curvature. Let a segment of passage be represented as:

When k(t) increased, the visitor leaned inward, anticipating. When k(t) softened, atten-

• Expansions, where curvature relaxed and the field opened.
These alternated with deliberate regularity, creating a rhythm more felt than seen.
