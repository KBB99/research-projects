Abstract
This paper studies Dynamic Alignment (Anantaprayoon et al., 2025), a scalable self-
improving alignment framework in which a Large Language Model (LLM) iteratively
aligns itself to a uniﬁed, open-ended alignment value called Collective Agency (CA) [2].
Dynamic Alignment comprises automated training data generation and a self-rewarding
mechanism implemented using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) [3]. We
argue that CA expresses a teleological alignment criterion that can be interpreted
as a coarse-grained operationalization of Simulated Agency (SA), and that GRPO-
based self-alignment constitutes a discrete training instantiation of RSVPs entropic
and agentic dynamics.
1
Introduction
Recent progress in LLMs has intensiﬁed the challenge of aligning increasingly autonomous
models beyond human-labeled objectives such as helpfulness, harmlessness, or honesty [5, 6].
Dynamic Alignment introduces a self-improving mechanism and a uniﬁed alignment value,
Collective Agency (CA), deﬁned as the inﬁnite expansion of agency across spacetime [2,
1]. This paper examines Dynamic Alignment conceptually and mathematically, situating it
within Simulated Agency (SA) and the Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum (RSVP).

3
Collective Agency (CA)
Collective Agency is a holistic directional principle whose deep structure unfolds through
four inseparable aspects: Knowledge, Benevolence, Power, and Vitality. Superior responses
advance all four coherently and durably (Anantaprayoon et al., 2025, p. 2). CA is evaluated
as a single scalar score (0-5), not as separate rewards, preserving the entangled structure.
4
Dynamic Alignment Framework
Dynamic Alignment comprises two phases [1]:
1. Automated training data generation: a goal generator, prompt generator, and
prompt evaluator collaboratively produce open-ended non-linguistic task prompts sat-
isfying predeﬁned criteria.
2. Self-improving: the policy model generates G = 8 candidate responses for each
prompt, uses a system prompt to reason about CA, and assigns deterministic self-
rewards ri ∈{0, . . . , 5}.
During gradient update, the CA system prompt is excluded to encourage internalization
rather than prompt imitation.

Simulated Agency (SA)
Simulated Agency interprets agency as a recursive inference process that maintains and
expands an agent's capacity for meaningful action.
We argue that CA functions as the
operational alignment value that SA implicitly prescribes.
7
Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum (RSVP)
RSVP models cognition and agency using scalar potential Φ, vector ﬂow v, and entropy
S ﬁelds. We propose a conceptual mapping: CA's four aspects correspond respectively to
informational richness, uplifting ﬂows, realized capacity, and regenerative entropy regimes
in RSVP's entropic dynamics.
8
Uniﬁcation: CA as Operational Teleology
We interpret Dynamic Alignment as a discrete training instantiation of RSVPs teleologi-
cal ﬂow. GRPO implements a variational improvement mechanism relative to a baseline,
analogous to RSVPs agentic gradient ﬂows, while CA provides the alignment direction.
9
Implementation Blueprint
The current Dynamic Alignment prototype is trained on a static generated dataset (not
interactive curriculum) and uses GPT-oss-20b as base model (Anantaprayoon et al., 2025).
Future work includes online self-improvement, multi-agent oversight, curriculum progression,
and decomposition of CAs pillars for interpretability.
10
Evaluation
Dynamic Alignment evaluates CA alignment using GPT-4.1 as judge on a held-out prompt
set, and tests preservation of general NLP capabilities on IFEval, AIME 2025, and GPQA
Diamond [10, 11]. CA-aligned models outperform base on CA judgments while maintaining
comparable performance on standard NLP benchmarks (Anantaprayoon et al., 2025).

11
Discussion
Dynamic Alignment presents a scalable, open-ended alignment paradigm that transcends
static preference labels, potentially mitigating reward hacking and epistemic capture. In-
tegrating CA, SA, and RSVP provides a framework for teleological alignment based on
sustaining and expanding agency across agents.
12
Conclusion
We conclude that Dynamic Alignment oﬀers a promising, scalable approach to alignment,
and that CA may serve as a physically and cognitively grounded teleological objective within
the broader RSVP ontology.
