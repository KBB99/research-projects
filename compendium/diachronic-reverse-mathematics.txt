A d-dimensional 
manifold, denoted as Q,

adhering to certain

directional derivatives

This tangent space, 
denoted as TqQ, is 
formed by the linear 
span of vectors 
∂φ⁻¹/∂xi. Similar to a 
tangent line to a curve, 
TqQ represents a copy of
Rd joined to Q at point q. 
A point v ∈ TqQ is 
referred to as a tangent
vector. This setup is 
illustrated in Figure 1.1.

2.1 Introduction
In this chapter, we 
introduce geodesic 
Lagrangian Monte Carlo 
(gLMC), a methodology for
Bayesian inference on a 
wide range of Riemannian 
manifolds. We employ the
space of positive definite

(PD) matrices as a 
concrete example to 
illustrate this approach. 
The resulting algorithms 
enable direct inference 
on the PD matrix space, 
marking a pioneering 
effort in this direction. 
gLMC facilitates more 
informative prior 
specification for 
covariance matrices, 
eliminating the need for 

conjugate priors and 
complex transformations 
on variables.
Positive definite matrices
are frequently 
encountered in statistics 
as covariance matrices 
that parameterize the 
multivariate Gaussian 
model. This model forms 
the basis of modern 
statistics and machine 

learning, impacting 
various areas such as 
linear regression, 
probabilistic principal 
component analysis, 
Gaussian Markov random 
fields, spectral density 
estimation, and Gaussian 
process models. The d-
dimensional Gaussian 
distribution is entirely 
defined by a mean vector
µ and a covariance matrix

Σ within S+d, the space 
of d-by-d positive 
definite matrices. By 
introducing various 
structures to the 
covariance matrix, 
diverse models can be 
constructed. While some 
cases permit 
parameterizing covariance
matrices using a few 
parameters, the learning
of unstructured 

covariance matrices, 
crucial for inference 
with a large number of 
parameters, remains 
challenging. Conventional
approaches like the 
conjugate Gaussian 
inverse-Wishart model 
have limitations. Other 
techniques involve non-
linear parameterizations,
but a means to perform 
direct Bayesian 

inference on the space 
of positive definite 
matrices using flexible 
prior specifications 
remains absent.
The central difficulty 
lies in sampling from a 
posterior distribution on 
a high-dimensional 
manifold with a 
boundary. To address 
this, our method 

capitalizes on the 
intrinsic Riemannian 
geometry of the PD 
matrix space. This space 
is incomplete under the 
Euclidean metric, where 
moving along a straight 
path may lead to non-
positive definite 
matrices. However, the 
space is geodesically 
complete under the 
canonical metric. 

Regardless of how far 
the sampler traverses 
along any geodesic, it 
never departs from the 
PD matrix space. This 
redefined notion of a 
"straight line" ensures 
that sampling remains 
within the desired set. 
The metric-defined 
geodesics offer a 
natural way to navigate 
the PD matrix space, 

aligning well with recent 
advancements in 
Hamiltonian Monte Carlo 
on manifolds.
Geodesic Lagrangian Monte
Carlo (gLMC) is employed 
for Bayesian inference, 
belonging to the class of 
Hamiltonian Monte Carlo 
algorithms. This 
methodology integrates 
numerical integration of 

a Hamiltonian system with
Metropolis-Hastings 
steps to maintain the 
invariance of the target 
distribution. It builds on 
Riemannian manifold 
Hamiltonian Monte Carlo 
(RMHMC), which utilizes 
second-order 
information through a 
Riemannian metric 
tensor. Lagrangian Monte 
Carlo (LMC) extends 

RMHMC by incorporating 
random velocity instead 
of random momentum, but
requires Jacobian 
corrections. Embedding 
geodesic Monte Carlo 
(egMC) considers 
parameter space 
geometry but is 
inapplicable when the 
Riemannian embedding of 
a manifold is unknown. 
gLMC, however, uses the 

split Hamiltonian 
formulation without 
necessitating an explicit 
Riemannian embedding, 
making it ideal for 
Bayesian inference on 
the space of PD matrices.
gLMC enables the 
treatment of covariance 
matrices akin to other 
model parameters, 
eliminating the need for 

conjugate priors or low-
rank structures. Its 
applications for PD 
matrices are 
demonstrated using 
simulated and real-
world data. First, gLMC's 
empirical outcomes align 
with the closed-form 
solution of the conjugate
Gaussian inverse-Wishart
model. Then, the 
application of gLMC to 

Hermitian PD matrices 
for multivariate 
spectral density 
estimation is highlighted. 
We compare results from 
different prior 
specifications, including 
the inverse-Wishart 
prior and complex 
reference prior. 
Credible intervals for 
squared coherences are 
obtained for simulated 

vector auto-regressive 
time series with known 
spectral density 
matrices. Lastly, gLMC is 
used to learn spectral 
density matrices 
associated with 
multivariate local field 
potentials (LFPs).
