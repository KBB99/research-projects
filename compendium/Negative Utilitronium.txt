Abstract
This paper develops the concept of Negative Utilitronium, a theoretical eco-
nomic state in which maximal well-being arises from minimal physical and infor-
mational activity. We propose a Spatial Subsistence Allocation (SSA) system where
immobility is ﬁnancially incentivized: individuals receive tiered payments propor-
tional to their conﬁnement radius ($200 for home-bound, $100 within 1 km, $50
within 100 km). Framed within thermodynamic, cybernetic, and degrowth litera-
tures, this model treats expenditure and entropy as conjugate variables. Through
diﬀerential formalization and simulation, we show how compensated stillness min-
imizes cost spiraling, stabilizes inﬂation, and converges toward a steady-state ne-
gentropic economy.
Negative Utilitronium is not an abolition of utility but its
inversion: pleasure as rest, agency as restraint, and motion as externality.
1
Introduction: From Growth to Stillness
Modern economies remain tethered to the ideology of perpetual motionmeasured through
GDP, consumption velocity, and supply-chain throughput. Yet, empirical crises ecolog-
ical overshoot, inﬂationary bubbles, and psychosocial exhaustion suggest that activity
itself has become the principal cost center.
1

This study inverts the growth paradigm. Instead of stimulating motion through ﬁs-
cal expansion, we propose paying for stillness. The concept of Negative Utilitronium
describes an asymptotic limit where societal utility is maximized when total systemic
energy expenditure approaches a minimum, constrained by cognitive and physiological
sustenance.

Explaining Key Concepts
To ensure clarity, we deﬁne several foundational concepts that underpin the Negative
Utilitronium framework, particularly those that may not be immediately self-evident.
2.1
Entropy in Economics
Entropy, borrowed from thermodynamics, refers to the measure of disorder or unpre-
dictability in a system [1].
In economic terms, it manifests as inﬂationary volatility,
resource ineﬃciency, and wasteful activity. Negative Utilitronium seeks to minimize this
entropy through compensated stillness, treating economic motion as a source of dissipa-
tive chaos.
2.2
Negentropy and Reversible Computation
Negentropy is the inverse of entropyorganized information or order that counters disorder
[5]. Drawing from information theory, reversible computation minimizes energy dissipa-
tion in processes [7]. Here, stillness acts as an economic analogue, where minimal activity
preserves value without wasteful erasure.
2.3
Spatial Subsistence Allocation (SSA)
SSA is the proposed universal basic income mechanism tied to immobility. Unlike tra-
ditional UBI, which decouples income from work [10], SSA explicitly rewards restraint,
aligning with degrowth principles of self-limitation [8].

Industrial Research Waste: Entropy in Innovation
Industrial research and development (R&D) exempliﬁes entropic ineﬃciency, generating
vast waste through redundant experimentation and failed prototypes. Global industrial
waste generation reached approximately 292.4 million tons in 2018, with manufacturing
sectors contributing signiﬁcantly to this ﬁgure [12]. Statistics indicate that manufacturing
waste increased from 2014 to 2018 before a temporary decline during the 2020 pandemic,
4

only to rise again [13].
In the context of Negative Utilitronium, such waste represents unchecked motion:
overproduction of knowledge artifacts without regard for systemic entropy. For instance,
the pharmaceutical industry discards billions in unused compounds annually, while tech
R&D produces electronic waste at rates exceeding 50 million tons per year globally [14].
Compensated stillness would redirect incentives toward minimal viable innovationprior-
itizing simulation over physical prototyping and collaborative knowledge sharing over
competitive duplication.

Mandatory Recycling: Enforcing Negentropic Cy-
cles
To operationalize Negative Utilitronium, we mandate universal recycling of all garbage,
transforming waste into a closed-loop resource. Examples of such policies include Cali-
fornia's mandatory commercial recycling ordinance, which requires businesses generating
four cubic yards of waste weekly to recycle [16], and Pittsburgh's requirement for recy-
cling paper, cardboard, plastics, and metals [17]. At a global scale, the European Union's
Waste Framework Directive enforces separate collection and recycling targets [18].
In this framework, non-compliance incurs entropy penaltiesﬁnes proportional to un-
recycled massensuring that material ﬂows align with thermodynamic eﬃciency.
This
mandate reduces industrial entropy by 40-60% in modeled scenarios, converting dissipa-
tive outputs into negentropic inputs [15].

Rest Economy, where cost, motion, and desire reach thermodynamic parity.
