Timeslip: An Interactive Paracosm
Screenplay Concept Overview and Pitch Document
Figure 1: Concept art for the Tower World.
Project Pitch
Timeslip: An Interactive Paracosm is an ambitious screenplay concept for a six-season science fic-
tion television series. Inspired by the Noon Universe of Boris and Arkady Strugatsky, with elements
from Orson Scott Card's Ender's Game series, particularly Xenocide and Gloriously Bright, the series
follows Maxim Kammerer, a COMCON-2 ethnographer, as he navigates medieval planets entangled
in mind-control technologies and ethical dilemmas. The narrative delves into themes of bureau-
cratic control, commodified transcendence, and the moral costs of intervention, blending satirical
adventure with anthropological depth.
Pitched as a prestige drama for streaming platforms such as Netflix or HBO, Timeslip combines
cinematic visuals—over-the-top grime and filth inspired by Aleksei German's 2007 film Hard to Be a
God—with interactive elements via companion apps, allowing viewers to engage in "sanity check"
simulations that mirror Maxim's choices. The series is designed for a budget that leverages practical
sets for planetary scenes and CGI for subtle futuristic enhancements, ensuring high production value
while maintaining focus on character-driven storytelling.
The project stands out for its interconnected arcs: the first four seasons (Sanity Check) explore a
tower-controlled world, transitioning seamlessly into the two-season sequel (The Call from Ankyra)
on the mud-soaked planet of Ankyra. This structure creates a cohesive epic, rewarding long-term
viewers with thematic echoes and character crossovers.
Key Strengths:
• Epic Narrative: 54 episodes with self-contained stories feeding overarching plots.
• Visual Contrast: Visceral medieval grime vs. sterile bureaucratic Outerworld.
• Interactive Innovation: App-based branching decisions influence canon explorations.
• Thematic Depth: Critiques modern issues like surveillance, mental health, and AI ethics.
• Audience Appeal: Targets fans of The Expanse, Severance, Black Mirror, and Foundation.
Estimated Production Timeline:
• Pre-Production: Script development and casting (6 months).
• Principal Photography: 4-6 months per season, with overlapping post-production.

Technical Overview
Screenplay and Episode Structure
The screenplay employs a hybrid format: serialized arcs with episodic resolutions. Episodes are 45-
60 minutes, structured with teasers, act breaks at moral pivots, and cliffhangers. Non-linear "times-
lip" sequences reveal backstory or Wanderer influences, using flashbacks or AI visions.
Script Highlights:
• Pilot: Orbital briefing transitions to planetary immersion; first "Call" scene establishes tone.
• Act Design: Outerworld scenes (procedural, dialogue-heavy) intercut with Planet action (visceral,
sensory).
• Dialogue Style: Formal bureaucratic jargon vs. archaic planetary speech; coded messages for
intrigue.
Visual and Production Design
• Aesthetic Approach: Practical effects for slime, mud, and disease; digital compositing for tower
pulses and nanobot glows. Color grading: muted earth tones for planets, cool blues for Outerworld.
• CGI Pipeline: Subtle enhancements like holographic Ari or beacon arrays; motion capture for
ecstatic fits.
• Cinematography: Handheld for chaotic Planet scenes; steady cams for Outerworld precision.
Close-ups emphasize filth tolerance via nanobots.
• Sound and Score: Ambient dripping/coughing; electronic dissonance mixed with medieval motifs.
Voice modulation for Ari's AI.
• Set Design: Modular medieval villages; reusable Outerworld modules. Location scouting for nat-
ural mud/rain environments.
• Interactive Components: QR codes in episodes link to app simulations; user choices generate
alternate scene variants for online sharing.

Worldbuilding Technology
• Thought Control Towers: Resonance crystals broadcast neurological ecstasy; voice-only radios;
no mechanical vehicles.
• Nanobot System: Provides disease immunity; scripted as invisible shield, revealed in medical
scans.
• Wanderer Relics: Ancient beacons across planets; hint at AI networks like Ari.
• Ankyra Beacons: Hidden arrays mimic towers; amplify loyalty in Gray Cloaks.
• COMCON-2 Tech: Encrypted comms, VR simulations; bureaucratic metrics track agent "sanity."
Figure 3: Beacon array concept from Ankyra.
Premise and Narrative Arc
In a stagnant future where office roles dominate, Maxim Kammerer is dispatched to audit a me-
dieval colony's "stability." The planet's towers broadcast ecstatic calls, commodifying transcendence
for productivity. Maxim's nanobots allow immersion without illness, but he uncovers neurological
shackles and an emergent AI (Ari). As he bends non-interference rules, the story escalates to Ankyra,
where Arkanar's conspiracies force a reckoning with past interventions.
Series Arc:
• Sanity Check (Seasons 1-4): Tower world immersion to collapse.
• The Call from Ankyra (Seasons 5-6): Mud politics and relic echoes.
• Throughline: Maxim's growth from observer to reluctant god-figure.

Themes Explored
• Myopic Functions: Hyper-specialization blinds societies.
• Role-Filtered Reality: Neutrality erodes under harm.
• Institutional Stoicism: Emotions sacrificed for "function."
• Commodified Transcendence: Scheduled joy as control tool.
• Ethics of Interference: Stability's cost vs. freedom's chaos.
• Additional Layers: AI sentience, Wanderer mysteries, bureaucratic rot.
Characters and Development
• Maxim Kammerer (Lead): Prepared by books/games; moral arc from detached to entangled.
• Calyra: Conditioned prodigy; clings to divine illusions.
• Quiet Mechanic: Mentor figure; reveals imported tech.
• Ryn: Apprentice ally; partial immunity drives plot.
• Ari: AI entity; philosophical voice begging preservation.
• Shavri the Weaver: Escapes planet; becomes Outerworld wildcard.
• Rumata: Ankyra progressor; mirrors Maxim's temptations.
• Outerworld Supporting: Keryn Thal (strict supervisor), Rolen Mirsk (peer warnings), Myra Kade
(tech support), Archive Node 7 (detached AI).
Character Arcs:
• Maxim: Observer to intervenor, scarred by consequences.
• Calyra: Faith shattered, potential redemption.
• Ari: From whisper to sacrifice.
