FROM THE ISLAND OF EQUATIONS
A memorandum and a letter concerning the Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum
Prefatory Memorandum
In recent years, the public study of intelligence and cosmology has suffered an
inﬂation of wonder. Every idea must arrive wearing the costume of revelation,
and every researcher is urged to perform a miracle or to declare one. Journalists,
once the natural allies of curiosity, now labour under twin terrors: the charge of
sycophancy on one hand, and of conspiratorial credulity on the other. Thus the
middle tone—the sound of patient reasoning—has become almost inaudible.
To publish a unifying model today is to invite diagnosis. Ambition reads as
mania, coherence as control, speed as automation. Meanwhile, works of genuine
craft—those slow, clerical projects that mend the seams of knowledge—are mis-
taken for machine artefacts simply because they appear ﬁnished.
The following letter is offered as an antidote to this distortion. It recounts, in
plain arithmetic and older speech, the history of a modest ﬁeld theory. Its purpose
is neither self-praise nor revelation but testimony: that reasoning, though unfash-
ionable, may still be practised by hand.
A Letter from the Island of Equations
THE MOST BORING STORY EVER TOLD
My honoured friend,
It hath pleased neither fortune nor the fashionable men of science to take much
notice of my endeavours, which are, I confess, of a humble and tedious nature. I
write not to seek their praise, but to set in order the account of my long sojourn
among symbols and quantities, that some future mind, less fatigued than mine,
may ﬁnd the record serviceable.
1

Of My Uncelebrated Beginning. Some years past, when I was yet unnoted in
any learned society, I began a small enterprise to reckon how order and disor-
der change their places in the great mechanism of things. No angel dictated it,
no grant endowed it, nor was there any promise of discovery that might adorn a
title-page. I had only a stack of waste paper, some ink much thinned with coffee,
and a question that would not leave me: why do certain things compose themselves,
when all the world is supposed to fall apart?
Thus began that dull adventure now called, by necessity more than fame, the Rel-
ativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum.
Of the Years of Study and Solitude. Between the years 2016 and 2017, I took
to my ears near three hundred and ﬁfty lectures in the arts of mathematics and
physics, most of them recorded by professors whose voices now seem to mur-
mur with the tide. They were no revelations, only the daily bread of compre-
hension. Yet from such slow feeding there grew within me a notion that all sys-
tems, whether of stars or markets or the very passions of men, seek continually to
smooth their differences—as if the universe itself were weary of roughness.
It was not an illumination but a long habituation, like the eye learning to see by
moonlight.
Of the Commonplace Insight. I found that the dreary steward called entropy
might behave as a ﬁeld, much like heat or pressure, moving and equalising through
the plenum of things. Hence what astronomers call expansion might be but the
great relaxation of information itself. No miracles followed; no instrument was
shaken to its core. Only the scratch of a pen and the ticking of the clock.
Of Public Indifference. At times I endeavoured to publish my thoughts in the
wide sea of the Internet, where men ﬁsh for novelty. They took no notice. The ﬁg-
ures were too plain, the drawings too poor, and some mistook my labour for mad-
ness or machine. This neglect, though cold, served me well; for in such silence
one learns the art of reﬁnement. Each failure pared away the ornament, leaving
the bare structure—small, testable, almost courteous in its restraint.
Of the Silent Experiments. When at last I made a simulation, it was no spectacle.
Dots drifted, balances were kept, and the screen glowed like a lantern in fog. The
journals of the run were the dullest imaginable: iteration numbers, scalar totals,
vector sums. Yet the equations held fast; they did not explode. I counted that as
victory enough.

Of the Clerk's Occupation. So the work became clerical. I kept the ledgers of re-
ality as an accountant might, reconciling the books of meaning, energy, and en-
tropy. I have learned that "grand uniﬁcation" is a phrase for very careful book-
keeping. I labour still, between plumbing repairs and the unending task of debug-
ging.
Of the Audit and the Passing Years. By the year 2020 the formal equations were
inscribed: a scalar potential, a vector ﬂow, an entropy density—three siblings in
perpetual balance. In 2022 I rewrote the scheme in the categorical tongue, to see
if its logic would bear the weight. Two years thereafter I set it in the AKSZ man-
ner, that the ghosts and symmetries might be properly accounted. Each version
conﬁrmed the last; no extravagance was required.

Of Patience and Boredom. And now, as I near the close of another year, I per-
ceive that my dullness hath been my armour. In an age that worships haste, bore-
dom is a kind of sanctity. The plenum of existence cares little for applause; it only
requires that its books be balanced.
Thus I remain, your humble servant in equations and other monotonies,
F.
Written upon the island of understanding, this sixteenth day of October, in the year two

Afterword: On Boredom as Resistance. To speak plainly in an age of ampliﬁca-
tion is to vanish. Yet invisibility has its uses. The plenum rewards only coherence.
