Abstract
This monograph formalizes and extends Barenholtz's conjecture that natural com-
putational systems—language, cognition, biological organization, and physical dynam-
ics—operate via autogenerative dynamics: self-encoding, non-Markovian processes
whose trajectories depend essentially on historical structure. The text unifies infor-
mation geometry, rough path theory, sheaf and topos theory, higher gauge geometry,
and renormalization to propose a coherent mathematical and experimental framework
for studying generativity across domains. Each Part develops deeper correspondences
between linguistic, biological, and physical systems, culminating in a unified theorem
linking cohomological flatness, energetic smoothness, and informational persistence,
followed by a concrete research proposal.

Prelude: Historical and Conceptual
Foundations
1.
From Shannon to Barenholtz
Information theory classically emphasizes memoryless channels and stationary statistics.
Autogeneration re-centers time and structure: generativity is not a state-to-state jump but
a trajectory traversing an internally learned space. This framing complements cybernetics,
dynamical systems, and the modern geometry of learning (amari2016info).
2.
Phenomenology of the "Pregnant Present"
Cognition binds past into a living present. Smooth continuation is not reducible to a finite-
order Markov boundary; coherence depends on historical entailments and constraints that
unfold along the path.
3.
Autogeneration: Four Pillars
1. Autogeneration: continuation rules are learned and encoded in topology/geometry of
the representational space;
2. Non-Markovianity: essential dependence on paths, not snapshots;
3. Smoothness: low-energy geodesic flow under information-geometric metrics;
4. Persistence: memory integration that maintains coherence across scales.
4.
Unifying Prior Work
Free-energy formulations (friston2019freeenergy), rough path analysis (lyons1998roughpaths),
higher categorical semantics (lurie2009htt), and mirror-symmetric dualities (kontsevich1994mirror)
point toward a common structure: coherence emerges when local predictions are globally
integrable, curvature is controlled, and entropy budgets close.
5.
Programmatic Commitments
We aim to: (i) define autogeneration rigorously; (ii) quantify non-Markovian memory; (iii)
connect local-to-global via sheaf cohomology; (iv) tie smoothness to energetic criteria; (v)
validate across language, biology, and physics; (vi) open-source measuring/visualization
infrastructure.

Definition 42.1 (Autogenerative Functor). Let Seq be the category of finite sequences with
prefix inclusions. An endofunctor A : Seq →Prob maps each sequence to a continuation law

are pullbacks.
Proposition 42.1 (Natural Sheaf Equivalence). Every autogenerative functor defines a lan-

natural isomorphism.

Part X
Research Proposal: A Five-Year
Experimental Theory of Autogeneration
48.
Executive Summary
Autogenerative dynamics unify language, cognition, biology, and physics under a single
geometric principle: coherent continuation is geodesic flow on a learned, non-Markovian
manifold. We propose a $4.7 M, 5-year program (2026-2030) to (i) formalise the theory
in Parts I-IX, (ii) build the open-source **Autogeneration Observatory**, and (iii) deliver
cross-domain experimental proof of the Barenholtz Consistency Principle (Corollary in Part
IX). Deliverables: 40 peer-reviewed papers, 6 datasets (>10 TB), 3 software crates, and a
2030 monograph.
49.
Detailed Aims
Aim 1. Quantify memory depth via scalable NMIk and persistent sheaf barcodes.
Aim 2. Map the Fisher-Wasserstein geometry of 100 transformer families.
Aim 3. Prove H1 = 0 ⇐⇒geodesic ⇐⇒flat attention for three modality classes.
Aim 4. Measure higher-gauge curvature in human EEG and protein trajectories.
Aim 5. Establish invariant dictionaries: entropy flux ↔Ricci scalar ↔Zipf slope.
