Abstract
The Entropy-Bounded Sparse Semantic Calculus (EBSSC) provides a unified formalism for
integrating the geometric semantics of SpherePOP with the probabilistic control structure
of Latent Policy Selection. EBSSC treats thought, inference, and concept formation
as policy-driven evolutions on fields of meaning (semantic spheres) constrained by entropy
and sparsity budgets. We define the formal syntax, typing, and operational semantics;
prove entropy soundness and stability; present a sparse free-energy objective; and show an
explicit isomorphism between EBSSC dynamics and unistochastic transition operators. The
result is a compositional calculus where semantic coherence, cognitive economy, and physical
information constraints coincide under a single variational principle.

Introduction
Cognition, collaboration, and concept formation can be understood as constrained processes
operating over structured internal representations. The free-energy principle frames biological
agents as systems that minimize a variational bound on surprise [31, 32], an approach generalized
in active inference to include planning and epistemic action [34, 51]. Separately, sparse coding
and compressed sensing demonstrate that high-dimensional signals can be reconstructed from a
small number of active components if the basis and sparsity structure are appropriate [55, 23, 24].
However, cognitive theories emphasizing inference rarely formalize the structure of meaning
itself, while semantic knowledge systems lack a principled theory of action selection or uncertainty
minimization. This paper presents the Entropy-Bounded Sparse Semantic Calculus (EBSSC), a
computational and mathematical framework that unifies:
1. Structured semantic representations, modeled as semantic spheres, drawing inspira-
tion from information geometry [2, 7], semantic energy formulations [28], and category-
theoretic process models [19, 6];
2. Goal-directed cognition as policy selection, expressed as sparse variational inference
over latent action spaces [32, 34, 52];
3. Entropy-constrained semantic evolution, in which concept dynamics obey bounded
information growth, analogous to entropy production constraints in thermodynamic and
open systems [39, 30, 59].
EBSSC models a cognitive or communicative act as a policy-induced transformation of a
semantic field, constrained by:
(i) bounded semantic entropy, preventing representational drift,
(ii) sparse policy activation, enforcing computational and cognitive parsimony, and
(iii) type-governed compositionality, ensuring semantic well-formedness.
This results in a calculus which is simultaneously:
• variational, minimizing a free-energy objective [31, 17];
• sparse, selecting minimal policy support [55, 23];
• semantic, maintaining structured concept geometry [2, 28];
• categorical, forming a bounded monoidal category of semantic morphisms [45, 44, 8];
• information-theoretic, admitting unistochastic transition semantics [10].
2.1
Motivating Gap
Three limitations motivate the construction of EBSSC:
1. Free-energy models lack semantic state structure. Active inference models treat
hidden states as latent variables but do not specify the geometry or compositional structure of

rather than others.

tive updates can accumulate unbounded noise or inconsistency, violating long-term conceptual
coherence [6, 39].
