Introduction
Entropys Edge: The RSVP Wars is a 4X (Explore, Expand, Exploit, Exterminate)
strategy simulation based on the Relativistic Scalar Vector Plenum (RSVP) cosmology. The
project integrates mathematical physics, cognitive thermodynamics, and ethics simulation
into an interactive computational universe. In RSVP theory, the universe is conceptualized
as a ﬁxed plenum governed by three interacting ﬁelds:
Φ scalar potential or semantic capacity (negentropic density), ⃗v vector ﬂow (energy
or baryon current), S entropy ﬁeld (gradient of disorder or informational smoothness).
Civilizations, cognition, and cosmology are all manifestations of these ﬁeld interactions.
Entropys Edge visualizes this plenum evolution as a turn-based game, where players man-
age ﬁeld gradients to achieve strategic objectives. The simulation emphasizes the interplay
between local negentropic structures and global entropic equilibration, reﬂecting principles
from non-equilibrium thermodynamics and information theory. The game mechanics are
derived directly from the underlying ﬁeld equations, ensuring that gameplay decisions have
predictable yet emergent physical consequences. This document provides a comprehensive
speciﬁcation, including expanded mathematical derivations, discretization schemes, stability
analyses, and implementation details.

Theoretical Foundations of RSVP Cosmology
RSVP posits that cosmic expansion and cognitive growth are instances of the same entropic
smoothing principle. Space does not expand; rather, entropy diﬀerentials diﬀuse toward
equilibrium, driving the emergence of complex structures.
2.1
Field Ontology
The fundamental ﬁelds are deﬁned as follows:
Φ : Scalar ﬁeld representing semantic potential or negentropic density,

⃗v : Vector ﬁeld modeling directed energy ﬂow or baryon current,

These ﬁelds interact in a ﬁxed spatial plenum, with no underlying metric expansion. Instead,
apparent "expansion" arises from the diﬀusion of entropy gradients.
2.2
Connection to Prigogine's Dissipative Structures
The RSVP framework draws inspiration from Ilya Prigogine's theory of dissipative structures
in non-equilibrium thermodynamics. Prigogine, who received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry
in 1977, demonstrated that irreversible processes in open systems far from equilibrium can
lead to the spontaneous formation of ordered structures. These dissipative structures, such
as Bnard convection cells or oscillatory chemical reactions (e.g., the Belousov-Zhabotinsky
reaction), maintain their organization by dissipating energy and increasing overall entropy.

ics: players must balance expansion (creating gradients) with integration (smoothing), akin

Numerical Stability Examples
Consider a 1D simpliﬁcation of the Φ equation: ∂tΦ = κΦ∂xxΦ, discretized on a grid with
h = 1, κΦ = 0.5. The CFL condition gives ∆t < 0.25. - Stable case: ∆t = 0.2. Starting with

leading to exponential growth of numerical errors, manifesting as checkerboard patterns or
divergence. In practice, for the coupled system with λ = 0.1, γ = 0.2, adaptive time-stepping
can be used: monitor the maximum ﬁeld change per step and halve ∆t if it exceeds 10%.

12.2
Integration of Edelman's Neural Darwinism
Edelman's Neural Darwinism posits that brain development and function arise from a pro-
cess of somatic selection among diverse neuronal groups, analogous to natural selection. This
involves three key mechanisms: developmental selection (initial variability), experiential se-
lection (strengthening via use), and reentrant mapping (dynamic interactions). In the game,
this is adapted to model the evolution of technologies and species traits within the RSVP
plenum. Technology nodes and species parameters form "neuronal groups" that compete for
resources and coherence. Developmental selection occurs during scenario generation, creating
initial variability in traits and tech trees. Experiential selection happens through gameplay,
where successful adaptations (e.g., techs that reduce entropy eﬀectively) are ampliﬁed. Reen-
trant mapping is simulated via feedback loops between the ethics tensor, ﬁeld equations, and
decision-making processes, allowing dynamic reconﬁguration of strategies. This integration
enables emergent cognitive-like behaviors in AI factions, where "learning" occurs through
selection pressures tied to plenum ﬁelds, fostering realistic evolution of civilizations.

Discussion of Alignment Failures
Alignment failures in AI and game design often arise
from instrumental convergence, where agents pursue subgoals like resource hoarding that
lead to unintended consequences.
In AI, this can manifest as mesa-optimization, where
inner optimizers diverge from outer objectives. In games, it appears as exploit loops or
unbalanced economies. The RSVP grounding mitigates this by enforcing thermodynamic
penalties on convergence, but potential failures include: (1) parameter miscalibration allow-
ing C growth without V increase, (2) anomaly sources overriding descent, and (3) multiplayer
interactions breaching the contraction assumption. These are addressed by adaptive tariﬀs
and robustness checks in simulations.
