Technics of Desire: Ellul, Rollins, and the Manufacture of Consciousne
Abstract. Jacques Ellul saw advertising as a machine that shapes not markets but
minds. Helen Rollins sees cinema as a psychic engine that frames what desire can
mean. Both trace how modern media turn freedom into feedback---how persuasion becomes
infrastructure. This essay links Ellul's ``indoctrination into technique'' with
Rollins' ``psychocinema,'' showing that the drive for efficiency and the dream of
narrative share one hidden goal: to keep contradiction smooth enough to sell.
Desire and Device: Ellul, Rollins, and the Crafted Mind
(Dvorak-Optimized Version)
Abstract. Ellul named ads a creed of machines; Rollins frames film as dreamwork
for capital. Each reveals how technique trains feeling---how we learn to crave efficiency
and enjoy our own constraint. This piece parallels Ellul's ``indoctrination'' and
Rollins' ``psychocinema,'' reading both as engines that tune emotion to order.
