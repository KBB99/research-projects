Shaf S: Rigidity-Fields & Line-Bind Structures
The rigidity-field S assigns to each region U of the park-topolog the line-bind tensor sU, a value
in the stationary class. Each sU is indexed by its stanchion-mark, sU = ⃗σ(U), piecewise-constant
across the binding corridors, jumping sharply on approach to the queue-walls, like a pressure reading
spiking at choke-points.
The overlap law reads:
⃗σ(Ui ∩Uj) = ⃗σ(Ui)|ij + ⃗σ(Uj)|ji,
addition taken in the bind-group: closed, but deliberately non-cancellative, so that old rigidity
can only be redistributed, thinned, or smoothed—never truly erased in a single stroke.
Regions where ⃗σ accumulates are called choke-sets; their measure is logged automatically in
/sys/majik/choke.
A vanishing ⃗σ marks reversible terrain: the Morlocks call such zones "soft floor". Positive ⃗σ
increments mark where the old designers forced travelers through unidirectional compression.
