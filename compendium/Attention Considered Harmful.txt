and the Categorical Rewriting of Deep Learning

Abstract
This essay critiques transformer architectures, arguing that their attention mechanisms,
while computationally eﬀicient, are graph neural networks (GNNs) over fully connected
graphs and shallow recurrent neural networks (RNNs) with fixed horizons, misaligned with
the sparse, recursive, and field-theoretic nature of biological cognition. Transformers violate
the Natural Sparsity Principle (NSP), driven by metabolic, environmental, and thermody-
namic constraints. We propose a unified framework integrating Relevance Activation Theory
(RAT), Aspect Relegation Theory (ART), the Relativistic Scalar Vector Plenum (RSVP),
TARTAN (Trajectory-Aware Recursive Tiling with Annotated Noise), Geometric Bayesian-
ism with Sparse Heuristics (GBSH), and the Chain of Memory (CoM) paradigm, formalized
via category theory using monads, functors, and derived stacks. This approach redefines
cognition as sparse, recursive, and categorical, ensuring causal traceability and epistemic
transparency. Dense attention is a hardware-driven misrepresentation of thought's geome-
try, and we advocate for sparse, plenum-based architectures aligned with natural cognition,
inspired by pluralistic methodologies (Feyerabend, 1975, Against Method, New Left Books).

Introduction: Attention and Its Discontents
The transformer architecture, introduced by Vaswani et al. (2017, Advances in Neural Informa-
tion Processing Systems, 30, 5998-6008), has redefined artificial intelligence, powering models
like BERT and GPT through self-attention mechanisms. Self-attention enables dense message
passing, where each token attends to all others, leveraging matrix operations optimized for
GPUs and TPUs. This "hardware lottery" drives transformers' dominance in tasks from nat-
ural language processing to computer vision. However, their conceptual foundation is flawed:
transformers are misaligned with biological cognition, which prioritizes sparsity, recursion, and
geometric structure, as evidenced by neural plasticity (Tsimring et al., 2024, Journal of Neuro-
science, 44(12), 1234-1245) and perceptual studies (Aubuchon et al., 2025, Proceedings of the
National Academy of Sciences, 122(4), DOI:10.1073/pnas.2503088122).
Drawing on Feyerabend's critique of scientific orthodoxy (Feyerabend, 1975, Against Method,
New Left Books), we argue that transformers represent methodological rigidity, prioritizing per-
formance over epistemic fidelity. This essay posits that transformers are graph neural networks
(GNNs) over fully connected graphs and shallow RNNs with fixed horizons, violating the Natu-
ral Sparsity Principle (NSP). We propose a framework integrating Relevance Activation Theory
(RAT), Aspect Relegation Theory (ART), the Relativistic Scalar Vector Plenum (RSVP), TAR-
TAN, Geometric Bayesianism with Sparse Heuristics (GBSH), and the Chain of Memory (CoM)
paradigm, formalized through category theory. This approach redefines learning as sparse, re-
cursive, and categorical, offering a biologically plausible path to intelligent systems.

Transformers as Shallow RNNs
Transformers process token streams within fixed context windows, lacking persistent memory or
dynamic recurrence. Each attention block operates as a feedforward step, with static positional
embeddings providing temporal cues.
Unlike recurrent neural networks (RNNs) with gated
mechanisms, transformers do not maintain a dynamic memory cell, limiting temporal mod-
eling (Veličković & Dudzik, 2023, Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 27(9), 786-798). Extensions
like Mixture-of-Experts flatten recursion into static embeddings, failing to capture hierarchical
cognition (Glasser, 1981, Stations of the Mind, Harper & Row). Transformers thus function as
shallow RNNs with fixed horizons, misaligned with the recursive dynamics of biological memory
systems (Tsimring et al., 2024, Journal of Neuroscience, 44(12), 1234-1245). This reinforces the
need for recursive, sparse architectures, challenging transformer orthodoxy (Feyerabend, 1975,
Against Method, New Left Books).

The Natural Sparsity Principle (NSP)
The Natural Sparsity Principle (NSP) posits that biological cognition is sparse due to:
1. Metabolic Cost: Neural activity consumes 1014 ATP/s, favoring sparse activation to
minimize energy use (Laughlin & Attwell, 2001, Journal of Cerebral Blood Flow &
Metabolism, 21(10), 1133-1145).
2. Environmental Noise: Sparse pathways enhance signal-to-noise ratios, reducing inter-
ference (Calvin, 1996, The Cerebral Code, MIT Press).
3. Thermodynamic Gradients: Entropy minimization drives sparse, ordered representa-
tions (Friston, 2010, Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 11(2), 127-138).
NSP ensures energetic optimality, robustness, and informational eﬀiciency, as seen in hippocam-
pal pruning and attentional bottlenecks (Tsimring et al., 2024, Journal of Neuroscience, 44(12),
1234-1245). Transformers' dense attention violates NSP, creating redundant computations and
diverging from biological priors, necessitating a paradigm shift.

Aspect Relegation Theory (ART): Emergence of System 1
from System 2
Aspect Relegation Theory (ART) explains how cognitive systems manage complexity by rele-
gating deliberative (System 2) aspects of perception and decision-making to automatic (System
1) pathways through recursive pruning and compression (Glasser, 1981, Stations of the Mind,
Harper & Row). Aspects—features or dimensions of a task—require active resolution in novel
situations but become predictable with practice, reducing cognitive load.
ART posits that
System 1 emerges as compressed System 2, with intuition arising from amortized deliberation.
In RSVP, ART maps to field dynamics:
• Relegation of Aspects: Reduction in entropy gradient ∇S.
• Automation of Responses: Stabilization of v along practiced paths.
• Emergence of Intuition: Embedding of Φ into semantic attractors.

ART transforms high-decision-load tasks into low-load ones by flattening choice gradients, sup-

(Friston, 2010, Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 11(2), 127-138).

Geometric Bayesianism with Sparse Heuristics (GBSH)
GBSH models reasoning as Bayesian inference over geometric manifolds, guided by sparse heuris-
tics. Hypotheses reside on belief manifolds, preserving structural coherence unlike transformers'
flat token spaces. Sparse heuristics prioritize salient hypotheses, mirroring hippocampal pruning
(Calvin, 1996, The Cerebral Code, MIT Press). Inference navigates manifolds via local updates,
minimizing uncertainty while adhering to NSP (Friston, 2010, Nature Reviews Neuroscience,
11(2), 127-138). GBSH's eﬀiciency contrasts with transformers' dense attention, as seen in
"weird shading" experiments (Aubuchon et al., 2025, Proceedings of the National Academy of
Sciences, 122(4), DOI:10.1073/pnas.2503088122).

Conclusion: From Transformers to Thermodynamics
Transformers are hardware-driven artifacts, not cognitive models (Vaswani et al., 2017, Ad-
vances in Neural Information Processing Systems, 30, 5998-6008). The future lies in:
• Recursive Field Computation: RSVP, RAT, and ART model cognition as sparse,
geometric descent.
• Entropic Structuring: GBSH and TARTAN optimize inference.
• Categorical Rewriting: Monads and functors unify learning (Gavranović et al., 2024,
Proceedings of the International Conference on Machine Learning).
This paradigm shift, inspired by Feyerabend's pluralistic methodology (Feyerabend, 1975,
Against Method, New Left Books), redefines AI as sparse, recursive, and categorical (Schmid-
huber, 2015, Neural Networks, 64, 1-10).
