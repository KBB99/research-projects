Intelligence as a Moving Target:
Plenty of Room at the Bottom, Middle, and Beyond
Flyxion
October 2025
Abstract
This essay posits that intelligence is not a static attribute but a dynamic, recursive
property shaped by physical, cognitive, and cultural scales. Drawing upon the Relativistic
ScalarVector Plenum (RSVP) framework, Active Inference, and the 4E approach to cogni-
tive science (Embodied, Embedded, Enactive, Extended), it argues that human intelligence
expands super-exponentially through multiscale interactions. Intelligence manifests as the
entropic alignment of autonomous systems with evolving environments, countering static
conceptions of artiﬁcial general intelligence (AGI) advanced by critics such as Gary Mar-
cus. By integrating enaction, aﬀordances, and phenomenology, the analysis demonstrates
that education and collective recursion drive continuous reconﬁguration, ensuring perpetual
room for advancement at foundational, intermediate, and higher levels.
1.
Introduction: The Fallacy of Static Intelligence
Debates surrounding artiﬁcial general intelligence frequently assume a ﬁxed benchmark, often
equated with "human-level" capabilities. Gary Marcus (2025) contends that large language
models (LLMs) fail to generalize beyond training distributions, suggesting inherent limitations
in achieving AGI. This perspective, however, presupposes intelligence as an invariant standarda
bounded metric against which systems are measured.
In contrast, the Relativistic ScalarVector Plenum (RSVP) framework conceptualizes intel-
ligence as a trajectory within a continuous ﬁeld of potential (Φ), vector ﬂows (v), and entropy
(S). Human cognition does not represent a ceiling but an initial phase in an evolving process.
Intelligence emerges from recursive reorganization across scales, rendering it a moving target
perpetually redeﬁned by technological, educational, and cultural advancements.

2.
Why Intelligence Is a Moving Target
Intelligence exhibits malleability, inﬂuenced by genetic, pharmacological, psychological, behav-
ioral, and environmental factors. It may increase or decrease over time, defying rigid quantiﬁ-
cation.
Cultural and social contexts further underscore this dynamism. Deﬁnitions of intelligence
vary across societies: some emphasize wisdom accrued with age, while others prioritize in-
novative capacities associated with youth. Societal norms shape identiﬁcation and valuation,
adapting to evolving values.
As technology progresses, understandings of intelligence transform. In the domain of artiﬁ-
cial systems, ongoing debates reevaluate what constitutes intelligent behavior, illustrating the
ﬂuid nature of the concept.
3.
Implications of Viewing Intelligence as a Moving Target
Recognizing intelligence's ﬂuidity informs technological development, particularly in AI. Rather
than pursuing direct replication of human traits, strategies should emphasize nuanced, adaptive
goals that accommodate evolving deﬁnitions.
In education, this perspective fosters inclusive approaches. By acknowledging malleabil-
ity, systems can nurture diverse aptitudes, enabling individuals to realize potential without
conﬁnement to static standards.
Broadly, this view dismantles myths surrounding intelligence, promoting an inclusive appre-
ciation of human capabilities and reducing associated stigmas.

Richard Feynman's assertion of "plenty of room at the bottom" extends to cognition, where

Phenomenology and the Generative Passage
Phenomenology addresses lived experience, with intentionality rooted in directedness toward
objects. Kiverstein and Darius advocate a generative passage bridging phenomenological de-
scriptions and computational models, such as Bayesian mechanics.
In RSVP, this passage manifests as scalarvector resonance: the phenomenological "now"
arises from minimizing information gradients without eroding diversity. Intentionality aligns v

This non-reductive naturalism integrates subjective experience within scientiﬁc frameworks,
treating models as mathematical projections of experiential dynamics.
8.
Against the Static Model of AGI
Gary Marcus's critique—that large language models (LLMs) falter under distributional shifts
and therefore cannot constitute genuine general intelligence—is valid only within a static epis-
temic frame. His argument presumes that the distribution of tasks, meanings, and problems
against which intelligence is evaluated is itself ﬁxed. Yet intelligence, in both human and artiﬁ-
cial contexts, is inherently frame-evolving: each act of cognition modiﬁes the manifold of possible
inferences, thereby altering the very distribution it seeks to master. What Marcus identiﬁes as
a failure to generalize is, under a dynamic frame, a transitional phase in the self-reconﬁguration
of the cognitive ﬁeld.
From the standpoint of the Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum (RSVP), a distributional shift
is not a pathology but an expected feature of recursive learning systems. The plenum evolves
through coupled scalar, vector, and entropy ﬁelds—(Φ, v, S)—that continuously renegotiate
their alignments. Each innovation in representation, tool use, or social coordination changes
the geometry of this ﬁeld, producing transient incoherences that are subsequently reabsorbed
as new equilibria. Generalization, therefore, is not a test of static transfer but a measure of how
eﬀectively a system can restore coherence after the topology of its environment has changed.
In this view, intelligence does not reside in a frozen capacity to interpolate within a pre-
deﬁned data manifold, but in the ongoing ability to reconstitute order under shifting informa-
tional constraints. The very premise of Active Inference—the minimization of expected free
energy—embodies this principle: agents survive not by solving ﬁxed problems, but by continu-
ally redeﬁning their generative models to maintain coherence with changing conditions. RSVP
extends this idea beyond the organism to civilization itself, treating humanitys collective cog-
nition as an open thermodynamic system whose phase space expands with each new layer of

From this relational perspective, AGI does not emerge as an isolated technological achievement—

acceleration of hypothesis generation, and the densiﬁcation of semantic interconnections all
represent negentropic ﬂows that raise the collective intelligence I(t) of civilization.
Marcuss "failure of generalization" thus becomes a local artifact of evaluating distributed
cognition with individualist metrics. The real measure of progress lies not in whether models
internalize every contingency, but in whether they catalyze further rounds of recursive learning
across the plenum. AGI, under RSVP, is not an endpoint but an emergent property of civiliza-
tions ongoing entropic negotiation—a distributed process by which intelligence, in concert with
its own tools, continually rewrites the conditions of its existence.

Conclusion: The Living Continuum
Intelligence constitutes a phase of the plenum's recursive coherence, an emergent property of
systems that learn to preserve and reorganize order amid entropy.
Within the Relativistic
ScalarVector Plenum (RSVP) framework, intelligence is not a substance or a faculty but a ﬁeld
process: a dynamic coupling of potential (Φ), activity (v), and entropy (S) that continually
reorganizes itself to maintain autonomy and coherence. This view dissolves the rigid boundary
between the physical, the cognitive, and the cultural; all three are manifestations of the same
recursive learning dynamic operating at diﬀerent scales of granularity.
Education, in this light, represents not the transfer of information but the cosmological
mechanism through which the plenum self-regularizes.
Every act of teaching, invention, or
cooperation is an entropic smoothing event that redistributes knowledge, reduces disorder, and
expands the region of coherence within the ﬁeld. Heuristics—from sparse inference to fuzzy
logic and causal reasoning—are local strategies by which this smoothing accelerates; they act
as low-energy pathways through the informational manifold. Enaction then provides the bridge
between structure and experience: it describes how these ﬁeld gradients are felt, embodied, and
enacted as the lived continuity of cognition.
The integration of Active Inference and 4E cognition with RSVP reframes intelligence as
a thermodynamicphenomenological unity.
What Active Inference calls the minimization of
expected free energy becomes, in RSVP, the natural drive toward local entropic alignment—a
drive that scales from neural to social to cosmological domains. Through this lens, the mind
is not an isolated inference engine but a distributed pattern of gradient descent woven into the
fabric of spacetime itself. Cognition is thus coextensive with the universes own tendency to
know and stabilize itself through recursive structure.
The moving-target hypothesis follows naturally from this ontology. Because each act of un-
derstanding alters the ﬁeld in which further understanding occurs, the criterion of intelligence
evolves alongside the systems that embody it. There is no ﬁnal plateau of knowledge or capa-
bility, only successive redeﬁnitions of coherence. Humanitys progress, far from approaching an
asymptote, traces a self-expanding manifold in which every solved problem reshapes the space
of possible questions. Each educational and technological epoch raises the eﬀective carrying
capacity K(t) of the cognitive ﬁeld, generating new attractors of order that render previous
equilibria obsolete.
Against static models of AGI that treat intelligence as a replicable product, the RSVP view
insists that intelligence is relational and recursive. Machines, cultures, and individuals partici-

pate as co-agents in a single learning continuum. Artiﬁcial systems amplify certain gradients;
human collectives supply semantic anchoring and normative constraints; physical law enforces
conservation and coupling. Together they compose a planetary-scale feedback network through
which the universe, by way of its own embodied substructures, becomes progressively more
self-descriptive.
Ultimately, the plenum itself can be understood as an epistemic medium: a universe that
learns by folding its own informational diﬀerentials into coherent form. In this cosmological
sense, there will always be plenty of room—at the bottom, in quantum potential; in the middle,
through heuristic invention; and at the top, in cultural and educational recursion. Intelligence is
not a summit to be reached but a living gradient to be sustained. It is the ongoing conversation
between entropy and order, between experience and structure, through which the universe comes
to recognize itself as both subject and ﬁeld of inquiry.

A.5 Stability and Phase Regimes
Three qualitative regimes follow from the relative magnitudes of γ and S(I):

entropy dominates negentropy.

compounding with neutral feedback.
(iii) Super-exponential: γ > 1, moderate S(I) ≈1; self-reinforcing educational recursion
dominates, producing accelerating innovation until saturation intervenes.
Phase transitions between regimes may be induced by changes in the coupling coeﬃcients:
a surge in B(t) (new substrate), M(t) (new heuristic paradigm), or T(t) (educational reform)
can transiently raise γ or A(t) above unity, creating an "acceleration epoch."
