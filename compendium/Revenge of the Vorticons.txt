redeﬁnes nested environments, offering design implications and research direc-

Commander Keen and the Vorticons' Miscommunica-
tion
The Commander Keen: Invasion of the Vorticons trilogy features an overworld map grant-
ing access to discrete levels, mirroring nested shell environments in computational
systems like bash. The narrative revolves around Billy Blaze, as Commander Keen,
countering the Vorticon invasion. A central theme is the Vorticons' misinterpretation
of Earth's transmissions, intending to interpret English messages encoded in Latin
letters. However, noise - manifesting as pixelation (graphical distortion in 1990s dis-
plays), bit ﬂipping (binary errors in data transmission), and entropy interference (chaotic
information degradation) - causes misinterpretation, rendering messages partially il-
legible. For example, in Marooned on Mars, Vorticon signs display distorted Latin-like
glyphs, such as a fragmented EXIT sign, symbolizing failed semantic decoding. This
aligns with RSVP's entropy ﬁeld (S), where high entropy disrupts cognitive coher-
ence, paralleling the challenges of maintaining state across recursive scopes. In The
Earth Explodes, the Vorticons' misreading of a transmission in the Washington D.C.
level leads to tactical errors, mirrored in the game's mechanics where players navigate
noisy environments to achieve clarity. In Keen Must Die!, the Vorticon Mothership
level features distorted instructions, amplifying gameplay challenges.
The game's mechanics formalize this recursive structure through navigation and in-
teraction:
1. Main Map as Global Scope: The overworld map represents the global bash
environment, maintaining persistent state (e.g., collected keys) while providing
entry to levels. Players navigate using the number pad, allowing movement up,
down, side-to-side, and diagonally, with no enemy encounters, reﬂecting a low-
S, high-Φ state in RSVP. This freedom of movement supports strategic planning,
aligning v vectors toward level selection without immediate threats.
2. Levels as Nested Scopes: Each level encapsulates local variables (e.g., Yorp ene-
mies, switches), isolated unless exported, mirroring bash scoping. Players move
side-to-side (left and right) using keyboard inputs, with gravity pulling down-
ward, requiring jumps or pogoing to reach higher platforms. Jumping allows
moderate height gains, while switching to the pogo stick enables higher jumps,
adjusting Φ as attention narrows to precise navigation. Gravity increases S by
constraining movement, reﬂecting local scope challenges. For example, in Ma-
rooned on Mars's Red Maze City, players jump to avoid Gargs or pogo to access
elevated switches.
3. Exits as Scope Resolution: Level completion returns control to the map, analo-

state. Exiting a level, such as the Vorticon Mothership, restores high Φ and
reduces S.
4. Save Points as Snapshots: Save points preserve game state, similar to reloading
.bashrc, enabling state restoration amidst noisy challenges.

Dreams as Imaginary Play and Generative Diffusion
Networks
In Commander Keen: Invasion of the Vorticons, dreams function as imaginary play, where
Billy Blaze, as Commander Keen, embodies a heroic alter ego in grand narratives of
saving Earth. This aligns with Piaget's (Piaget, 1962) theory of play, where children
construct imaginary scenarios to explore agency. Each level, such as the Vorticon
Mothership in Keen Must Die!, represents a recursive layer of this dream, embed-
ding challenges like navigating laser traps or defeating Vorticon Elites. The Vorticons'
misinterpretation of English transmissions reﬂects an error-prone generative diffusion
network - a cognitive model where dream states attempt to generate coherent text but
are disrupted by noise, akin to diffusion models in machine learning (Ho et al., 2020).
Cognitive science notes that text in dreams is often illegible or shifting due to neural
noise (Hobson, 2002). For example, in Marooned on Mars, a sign reading HELP appears
jumbled, mirroring dream text instability. In RSVP terms, entering a dream-like level
reduces the scalar ﬁeld (Φ), aligning the vector ﬁeld (v) toward heroic objectives, while
the entropy ﬁeld (S) increases due to diffusion network errors. Level resolution re-
stores high Φ and reduces S, akin to waking. This connects to Revonsuo's (Revonsuo,
2000) hypothesis of dreaming as threat simulation, where Keen's heroic role rehearses
agency.
The Ice City level in Marooned on Mars features slippery surfaces and Skree enemies,
creating a high-S environment that mirrors dream text instability. The First City
level's Vorticon signs, with fragmented Latin letters, further illustrate this, as players
struggle to decode them. Predictive coding (Friston, 2010) suggests the brain min-
imizes prediction errors in noisy dream states, paralleling Keen's navigation. The
default mode network (Domhoff, 2001) generates unstable narratives in dreams, re-
inforced by the sheaf-theoretic formalism of RSVP, where local cognitive modules
(dream levels) contribute to global ﬁeld dynamics, with noise amplifying S.

RSVP: Field Dynamics and Cognitive Navigation
4.1
Core Principles of RSVP
The Relativistic Scalar Vector Plenum (RSVP) theory posits that consciousness emerges
from recursive tiling and entropy modulation over nested vector ﬂows, characterized
by:
• Scalar Field (Φ): Represents attentional density, with high values for broad fo-
cus (e.g., overworld navigation) and low values for narrow focus (e.g., level chal-
lenges).
3

• Vector Field (v): Encodes semantic attractors, guiding cognitive orientation to-
ward goals (e.g., left-right movement or level objectives).
• Entropy Field (S): Tracks cognitive tension, increasing with novelty (e.g., en-
emy encounters, gravity constraints) and decreasing with resolution (e.g., level
completion).
These ﬁelds interact dynamically, creating localized perturbations that resolve into
global coherence.
4.2
Mapping RSVP to Commander Keen
In Invasion of the Vorticons, the overworld map corresponds to a high-Φ state, with
diffuse v vectors (diagonal and orthogonal movements) and low S due to the absence
of enemies. Entering a level, such as the Red Maze City in Marooned on Mars, reduces
Φ as attention narrows to side-to-side movement, jumping, or pogoing to avoid Garg
enemies and reach elevated platforms, aligns v toward collecting a pogo stick, and
increases S due to gravity and transmission noise. A Vorticon sign's garbled EXIT
text increases S, reﬂecting decoding challenges. Level completion restores high Φ,
disperses v, and reduces S. In Keen Must Die!, the Vorticon Mothership level involves
navigating laser grids with precise jumps or pogoing, amplifying S.
4.3
Attention Modulation
Attention modulation in RSVP parallels the player's shift between map navigation
(broad, free movement) and level challenges (constrained by gravity and enemies).
Cognitive research (Monsell, 2003) shows hierarchical transitions incur costs, reﬂected
in RSVP's Φ reductions. In Commander Keen, strategic map navigation contrasts with
tactical level focus, with noise - as in The Earth Explodes's Washington D.C. level -
challenging clarity.
4.4
Extended RSVP Analysis
RSVP's dynamics can be modeled with differential equations, where Φ(t) evolves as
dΦ/dt = −α(Φ −Φtarget), with α governing attention shift speed (e.g., from map
to level), and S(t) follows dS/dt = β(novelty −resolution), with β reﬂecting en-
tropy sensitivity (e.g., gravity or enemy encounters). In Marooned on Mars's Second
City, navigating Butler robots with side-to-side movement and pogoing increases S,
resolved by collecting a keycard, reducing S and restoring Φ. This connects to the Vor-
ticons' transmission errors, where noise ampliﬁes S, requiring cognitive resolution.
