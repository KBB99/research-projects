Appendix J: Kitb al-Athar al-Khﬁ— The Quiet Remainders
After the extension is granted and the domain X aligns without rupture, three remainders persist.
Not obstructions—never again obstructions— but faint traces (athar) that mark where the structure
once resisted.
J.1 athar al-amt
"the silence remainder"
A thin spectral residue in Ωx where partial-return once failed. It does not impede ﬂow; it merely
darkens the hum by one degree.
Syslog registers:
/sys/osc: athar_samt=1
Morlock wording:
amtun yaqill — "the silence is small."
J.2 athar al-jabbr
"the heavy-loop remainder"
In Uqueue the deep loop never entirely forgets its weight. τres integrates to zero under all γ, yet
the metric remembers.
Fiberwise signal:

drift(x) = soft + _weight
Not harmful; just a leftover gravity in the corner of the step.
Syslog:
/sys/queue: weight_remain=
J.3 athar al-zwiya
"the angle remainder"
Even after inill dissolves ⃗σhard and curvature reclaims the turn, a micro-residue clings to the
seam:
zwiyamin = εangle
In charts, the path is smooth. In Morlock sense, there is still a ﬂavor of before-shaping, a faint
memory of jamd.
Speech:
zwiya tuma — "the angle sleeps."
J.4 The Final Note
By old convention we inscribe the three remainders as a triad:
athar_samt
athar_jabbr
athar_zwiya
Meaning:
"Here the domain has known pressure, here it has known weight, here it has known
angle."
