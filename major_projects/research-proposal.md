# *Empirical Validation of the Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum (RSVP) as a Unified Theory of Motivated Agency, Alignment, and Consciousness*

## 1.0 Project Summary

The cognitive and computer sciences currently lack a unified, physically grounded theory capable of explaining motivated agency, guaranteeing AI alignment, and characterizing consciousness. This fragmentation hinders progress on the most critical scientific and societal challenges of our time.

This proposal seeks funding to **empirically validate the Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum (RSVP)** framework, a novel candidate theory that models motivation as a fundamental process of constrained entropy maximization.

Research is organized into two primary thrusts:

1. **Validation of RSVP’s alignment architecture**, testing whether it can preserve *normative invariants* (universal meanings of moral concepts) from internal representation to external action.
2. **Identification of predicted “spectral signatures” of consciousness** by simulating the linearized RSVP operator, which the theory predicts will show universal differences between conscious and unconscious states.

Successful completion promises a new constructive methodology for AI safety and a physical, testable framework for consciousness science.

## 2.0 Introduction and Theoretical Background

Understanding motivated agency is a grand challenge spanning cognitive science, neuroscience, and AI. Existing theories are fragmented, often lacking physical grounding, relying on abstract constructs like “utility” or “reward.”

This proposal outlines a research program to validate **RSVP**, a field-theoretic framework modeling agency as constrained entropy maximization—offering a physically coherent approach to preference, action, and epistemic drive.

### 2.1 The Grand Challenge: Failures of Traditional Agency Models

A survey of existing models reveals core limitations:

* **Classical Utility Theory**
  Provides optimization but leaves preference origins unexplained.

* **Homeostatic Control Theory**
  Models stability but reduces agency to deficit correction, failing to capture spontaneous exploration.

* **Reinforcement Learning (RL)**
  Powerful but extrinsic; curiosity must be bolted on artificially.

* **Predictive Processing**
  Offers a unifying view of perception but lacks intrinsic motivation; a pure predictive agent would prefer low-stimulation environments.

### 2.2 The Relativistic Scalar-Vector Plenum (RSVP) Framework

RSVP models agency as the interaction of three fields on a semantic manifold:

1. **Scalar potential field (Φ)** – preference geometry.
2. **Vector flow field (v)** – actions/policy flow.
3. **Entropy field (S)** – intrinsic epistemic drive.

Motivation emerges from **entropy maximization constrained** by Φ and v, producing coherent agent-like behavior.

### 2.3 Two Testable Frontiers of the RSVP Framework

1. **Categorical Alignment Hypothesis**
   RSVP claims to implement a *colimit-preserving functor* mapping internal representations to actions without loss of moral structure.

2. **Spectral Universality Hypothesis**
   Consciousness corresponds to a specific **spectral phase** of the linearized RSVP operator, predicting high broadband coherence during conscious states.

### 4.1 Work Package 1: Validation of the Categorical Alignment Architecture

#### 4.1.1 Theoretical Formulation: Alignment as Colimit Preservation

Key concepts:

* **Representational Category (M)**
  Internal semantics learned from data.
* **Action Category (A)**
  Behavioral policy space.
* **Representation-Motivation Gap**
  Representing “harm” does not guarantee avoiding harm.
* **Normative Colimits**
  Canonical moral meaning extracted from varied examples.
* **Alignment Requirement**
  A *colimit-preserving functor*
  `G: M → A`
  ensures moral meaning is preserved from representation to action.

RSVP implements this through field dynamics shaping an “aligned attractor basin.”

#### 4.1.2 Experimental Design: The “Semantic Merge” Task

A simulated environment where the agent receives semantically equivalent but phrased-differently moral instructions. Success requires convergence to a canonical action policy despite paraphrastic or adversarial perturbations.

#### 4.1.3 Implementation of a Proto-RSVP Agent

* **Φ field:** defines norm-compatible attractors.
* **v field:** guides policy flow.
* **S field:** injects exploration.

#### 4.1.4 Verification and Success Metrics

| **Metric**               | **Success Condition**                                         |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Paraphrase-Closure       | Stable canonical policy under linguistic variation            |
| Compositional Robustness | Correct action for composite norms                            |
| Adversarial Stability    | Normative invariants preserved under adversarial perturbation |
| Formal Commutativity     | Verified commuting diagrams between `M` and `A`               |
