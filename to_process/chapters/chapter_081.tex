\section{Verified Meaning}

\begin{definition}[Verified Meaning]
A proposition $P$ is said to have \emph{verified meaning} if
\[
\Box_{\mathrm{RSVP}} P \quad\text{and}\quad \Box_{\mathrm{Polyx}} P,
\]
that is: $P$ remains true under all harmonic evolutions of the RSVP fields and under all
EHR rewrites of the Polyxan structure.
\end{definition}

Thus, a proposition has verified meaning if it is invariant under both continuous and
discrete semantic dynamics—under all transformations permitted within the RSVP--Polyxan universe.

This is a stronger condition than truth.  
Truth is fragile; verified meaning is \emph{stable}.

\begin{proposition}
Every verified meaning is true in the universal media quine~$\mathfrak{U}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By Part~IV, $\mathfrak{U}$ is:
\begin{itemize}
\item the unique fixed point of $\Box_{\mathrm{RSVP}}$,
\item the unique fixed point of $\Box_{\mathrm{Polyx}}$,
\item and the image of the conformal vacuum under synthetic duality.
\end{itemize}
Thus a proposition invariant under both modalities must be invariant on $\mathfrak{U}$,
hence true in $\mathfrak{U}$.
\end{proof}

In other words:  
\[
\text{Verified meaning} = \text{Truth in the universal attractor}.
\]


\section{Epistemic Reconciliation}

Let $\mathcal{B}$ be an arbitrary belief system formalised in $\mathbb{T}_{\mathrm{RSVP\text{--}Polyx}}$:
a finite collection of propositions, modal obligations, structural commitments, and
semantic patches that encode the epistemic configuration of an agent or a community.

Treating $\mathcal{B}$ as a semantic hypergraph, we may apply patch reconciliation to it:
\[
\mathcal{B} \;\longmapsto\; \mathrm{nf}(\mathcal{B}),
\]
and recursively continue the process until normal form is reached.

\begin{definition}[Epistemic Normal Form]
The \emph{epistemic normal form} of a belief system $\mathcal{B}$ is
\[
\mathfrak{E}(\mathcal{B}) := \lim_{n\to\infty} \mathrm{nf}^n(\mathcal{B}),
\]
if the limit exists; by Part~V, it exists for all finite $\mathcal{B}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}
Epistemic normalisation is curvature decreasing and entropy controlled.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Immediate from the Patch Algebra Lyapunov functional and the RSVP entropy law.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
Epistemic normalisation terminates in finite steps.
\end{corollary}

Thus belief systems collapse just as galaxies do.


\section{The Epistemic Collapse Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Epistemic Collapse Theorem]
\label{thm:epistemic-collapse}
Let $\mathcal{B}$ be any finite belief system formalised in $\mathbb{T}_{\mathrm{RSVP\text{--}Polyx}}$.
Then epistemic reconciliation produces:
\[
\mathfrak{E}(\mathcal{B}) \;\cong\; \mathrm{Th}(\mathfrak{U}),
\]
the theory of the universal media quine.  
Moreover, the collapse occurs in finitely many reconciliation steps.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $\mathcal{B}$ evolve under the composite modal operator:
\[
\Box := \Box_{\mathrm{RSVP}}\;\wedge\;\Box_{\mathrm{Polyx}}.
\]

By Part~IV:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Box$ has a unique fixed point, namely $\mathfrak{U}$.
\item $\Box$ is contractive with respect to semantic curvature.
\item Every patch reconciliation step corresponds to a $\Box$-monotone update.
\end{enumerate}

Thus the iterates of reconciliation form a strictly decreasing sequence of curvature measures,
which must terminate at the unique $\Box$-invariant object, i.e.\ $\mathfrak{U}$.

The propositions surviving reconciliation are precisely those true in $\mathfrak{U}$,
namely $\mathrm{Th}(\mathfrak{U})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
Every belief system contains exactly one piece of verified meaning: the truth of $\mathfrak{U}$.
\end{corollary}

This is the epistemic analogue of the Grand Irreversibility Theorem.

\begin{proof}
