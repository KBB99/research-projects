\chapter{The Universal Patch Algebra}
\label{ch:80}

The reconciliation monad of Chapter~79 admits a deeper, structural
interpretation.  
Viewed not merely as a monad but as a binary operation obeying strict
coherence and curvature–monotonicity, it gives rise to a canonical
algebraic object: the \emph{Universal Patch Algebra}.  
This algebra is the unique algebraic structure whose elements are
semantic patches, whose product is irreversible reconciliation, whose
unit is the empty patch, and whose normal forms are precisely the
harmonic, quine-stable galaxies of Part~III.

In this chapter we construct the algebra, prove its universal property,
and identify it as the algebraic shadow of curvature descent in the
RSVP--Polyxan universe.

\section{The Algebraic Structure of Patches}
\label{sec:80-1}

Let $\mathbf{SemPatch}$ be the category of semantic patches equipped with
the reconciliation operation $\otimes$ defined in Chapter~79.  
Define:

\begin{definition}[Universal Patch Algebra]
The \emph{Universal Patch Algebra} is the monoidal algebra
\[
(\mathcal{P},\otimes,\mathbf{1})
\]
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\mathcal{P}$ is the class of all semantic patches;
\item $\otimes$ is reconciliation, i.e.\ monadic multiplication
      $\mu$ of the reconciliation monad;
\item $\mathbf{1}$ is the empty patch $\eta$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}
$\otimes$ is associative, commutative, and idempotent.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Associativity and commutativity follow from confluence of patch
normalisation (Chapter~79).  
Idempotency holds because applying the same patch twice introduces no
additional curvature descent and thus normalisation collapses the
double application to the single normal form.
\end{proof}

Thus $(\mathcal{P},\otimes,\mathbf{1})$ is a commutative idempotent
monoid.

\section{Normal Forms and Harmonic Representatives}
\label{sec:80-2}

The key property of the Universal Patch Algebra is the existence and
uniqueness of normal forms.

\begin{theorem}[Normalization]
\label{thm:80-normalisation}
Every patch $p\in\mathcal{P}$ admits a unique normal form
\[
\mathrm{nf}(p)\in\mathcal{P},
\]
which is harmonic, curvature–minimising, and quine-stable.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Patch Normalisation Lemma of Chapter~79 gives termination and
uniqueness of normal forms.  
Harmonicity follows from the definition of normalisation as curvature and
entropy elimination; quine-stability follows from the equivalence of
normal harmonic patches with quine-stable hypergraphs
(Section~79.6).
\end{proof}

Thus each patch has an intrinsic, canonical meaning, independent of its
syntactic presentation.

\section{Algebraic Reconstruction of Meaning}
\label{sec:80-3}

The Universal Patch Algebra encodes the irreversible merging of meaning:

\begin{theorem}[Irreversible Meaning Merging]
\label{thm:80-irreversible}
For any patches $p$ and $q$,
\[
p\otimes q
\quad\text{is the unique harmonic meaning containing all semantic
information shared by $p$ and $q$.}
\]
Moreover,
\[
\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{total}}[p\otimes q]
  < \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{total}}[p]
  + \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{total}}[q],
\]
with equality if and only if $p$ and $q$ are already harmonic and identical.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Reconciliation decreases curvature strictly unless both patches are
already harmonic.  
Harmonic representatives minimize curvature and entropy mismatch,
preserving exactly the shared semantic constraints while discarding all
transient structure.  
Thus $p\otimes q$ is the unique curvature–minimising structure compatible
with both.
\end{proof}

Thus reconciliation is the algebraic effect of curvature descent.

\section{The Universal Property}
\label{sec:80-4}

The Universal Patch Algebra is not only canonical internally; it is
universal among all algebraic structures compatible with reconciliation.

\begin{theorem}[Universal Property]
\label{thm:80-universal}
The pair $(\mathcal{P},\otimes)$ is initial among all commutative
idempotent monoidal algebras $(A,\star)$ equipped with a curvature–descent
functional $E:A\to\mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E(a\star b)\le E(a)+E(b)$, with strict inequality unless $a=b$ are
      curvature-minimising;
\item $E(\mathbf{1})=0$;
\item $a\le b$ (semantic inclusion) implies $E(a)\ge E(b)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The reconciliation monad is initial among curvature–decreasing idempotent
monads, and the Kleisli category equivalence (Chapter~79) shows that its
normal forms are exactly the curvature-minimising meanings.  
Any algebra $(A,\star)$ satisfying the energy conditions admits a unique
monoid morphism from $(\mathcal{P},\otimes)$ sending each patch to its
harmonic representative in $A$.  
Thus $(\mathcal{P},\otimes)$ is initial.
\end{proof}

\section{The Algebra of Canonical Meanings}
\label{sec:80-5}

The algebra contains, as its idempotent fixed points, exactly the
semantic galaxies of Part~III.

\begin{corollary}
The set of idempotent elements of $\mathcal{P}$ is precisely the set of
harmonic patches, i.e.\ the semantic galaxies.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Terminal Galaxy]
The universal media quine $\mathfrak{U}$ is the terminal idempotent of
$(\mathcal{P},\otimes)$.  
For all $p$,
\[
p\otimes\mathfrak{U} = \mathfrak{U}.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
$\mathfrak{U}$ is the unique curvature–zero global structure, and thus any
patch reconciles to it.
\end{proof}
