\section{Definition of Epistemic KL Divergence}

Given two semantic worlds $(\mathcal{M}_1,p_1)$ and $(\mathcal{M}_2,p_2)$,
define the Epistemic KL Divergence as:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:epistemic-kl-def}
D_{\mathrm{KL}}^{\mathrm{epi}}
   \bigl((\mathcal{M}_1,p_1)\,\|\,(\mathcal{M}_2,p_2)\bigr)
  :=
  \int_{\mathcal{M}_1}
    \log\!\left(\frac{dp_1}{dp_2\circ T}\right)
  \, dp_1,
\end{equation}
where $T$ ranges over admissible transport maps permitted by the synthetic duality functor $\mathbb{R}$.
The divergence is the minimal such value.

Thus epistemic KL divergence is the relative entropy between the full geometric--syntactic presentations of two semantic worlds.

\section{Properties of Epistemic KL Divergence}

\subsection{Monotonicity under RSVP flow}

If $\mathcal{M}(t)$ evolves under RSVP harmonic flow, then
\[
\frac{d}{dt}\,
D_{\mathrm{KL}}^{\mathrm{epi}}
\bigl((\mathcal{M}(t),p(t))\,\|\,(\mathcal{M}^\ast,p^\ast)\bigr)
\le 0,
\]
with equality only at the universal quine.
Thus epistemic distance decreases inexorably in time.


\subsection{Monotonicity under EHR rewriting}

If $\mathcal{G}\to\mathcal{G}'$ is an EHR rewrite, then
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}^{\mathrm{epi}}(\mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{G}} \,\|\,\mathcal{M}^\ast)
  \ge
D_{\mathrm{KL}}^{\mathrm{epi}}(\mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{G}'} \,\|\,\mathcal{M}^\ast).
\]

Discrete curvature reduction reduces epistemic divergence.


\subsection{Convexity}

For any mixture of semantic worlds
\[
(\mathcal{M}_\lambda,p_\lambda)
  = \lambda (\mathcal{M}_1,p_1) + (1-\lambda)(\mathcal{M}_2,p_2),
\]
KL divergence is convex in the first argument:
\[
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\mathcal{M}_\lambda \,\|\, \mathcal{M}_3)
  \le \lambda D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\mathcal{M}_1 \,\|\,\mathcal{M}_3)
  + (1-\lambda) D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\mathcal{M}_2 \,\|\,\mathcal{M}_3).
\]
