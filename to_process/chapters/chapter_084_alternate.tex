\chapter{Civilizational Phase Transitions and the Epistemic RG Flow}
\label{ch:84}

In this chapter we extend the epistemic geometry of
Chapter~\ref{ch:83} to the full temporal evolution of civilizations.
A civilization is represented as a stratified cognitive manifold
$\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{collective}}$ equipped with curvature
$\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{collective}}$, entropy $S_{\mathrm{collective}}$,
and an institutional Polyxan skeleton
$\mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{collective}}$ embedded by
$\iota_{\mathrm{collective}}$.

Historical change is then realised as an irreversible
\emph{epistemic renormalization flow}:
a canonical coarse-graining that eliminates unstable,
high-curvature belief structures and replaces them by
progressively more quine-stable, harmonic strata.

Civilizational phase transitions---collapses, renaissances, ideological
bifurcations, and large-scale integrations---appear mathematically as
critical phenomena in this epistemic RG flow.

\section{The Epistemic Renormalization Operator}

Let
\[
(\Phi_{\mathrm{collective}},
 \mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{collective}},
 S_{\mathrm{collective}};
 \mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{collective}},
 \iota_{\mathrm{collective}})
\]
denote the collective RSVP--Polyxan configuration introduced in
Chapter~\ref{ch:83}.  We now define the central coarse-graining operator.

\begin{definition}[Epistemic RG Operator]
The \emph{epistemic renormalization operator}
\[
\mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}} :
\mathcal{C}
\to
\mathcal{C}
\]
acts on the space $\mathcal{C}$ of all collective configurations by
\[
\mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}}
(\Phi,\mathbf{v},S; \mathcal{G},\iota)
=
(\Phi',\mathbf{v}',S'; \mathcal{G}',\iota'),
\]
where:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $(\Phi',\mathbf{v}',S')$ is the harmonic projection of
      $(\Phi,\mathbf{v},S)$ obtained by one step of curvature--entropy
      relaxation (RSVP flow at unit time),
\item $\mathcal{G}'$ is obtained from $\mathcal{G}$ by performing all\
      curvature-reducing EHR rewrites accessible within its current
      incidence radius,
\item $\iota'$ is the unique harmonic embedding into the relaxed
      geometry, guaranteed by the Embedding Regularity Theorem
      (Chapter~31).
\end{enumerate}

\end{definition}

Because each component of $\mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}}$ is energy-decreasing,
the operator is well-defined, terminating, and contractive.

\begin{proposition}[Contractivity]
There exists $\lambda<1$ such that for all collective states
$X,Y \in \mathcal{C}$,
\[
d\bigl( \mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}}(X),
        \mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}}(Y) \bigr)
\;\le\;
\lambda\, d(X,Y),
\]
where $d$ is any of the canonical synthetic-duality metrics.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each component of the map is a contraction:

\begin{itemize}
\item The harmonic projection on RSVP fields is strictly contracting
      by standard parabolic smoothing estimates.
\item The EHR subsystem decreases discrete curvature by at least $1$ at
      each step (Chapter~22), yielding Lipschitz constants $<1$.
\item The harmonic embedding is unique up to diffeomorphism and depends
      smoothly on the target fields, giving continuous dependence with
      Lipschitz constant strictly less than $1$ near any quine-stable
      state.
\end{itemize}

Combining these, standard product-metric arguments give the claimed
contractivity.
\end{proof}

\section{Civilizational Phases and Their Geometry}

\begin{definition}[Civilizational Phase]
A \emph{civilizational phase} is a connected region
$\mathcal{P}\subset\mathcal{C}$ on which
$\mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}}$ is smooth and strictly decreases
epistemic curvature:
\[
\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{collective}}(t+1)
<
\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{collective}}(t).
\]
\end{definition}

Within such a phase, history unfolds as slow relaxation.
Transitions between phases---collapses, ideological shifts, or
institutional reorganizations---are triggered by curvature exceeding a
universal threshold.

\begin{theorem}[Phase Transition Criterion]
A civilization undergoes an epistemic phase transition at time $t$ iff
\[
\sup_{x\in\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{collective}}}
\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{collective}}(x,t)
\;\ge\;
\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{crit}},
\]
where $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{crit}}$ is the smallest curvature at which
\emph{no quine-stable embedding is compatible} with the local entropy
and type-sheaf constraints.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
If curvature surpasses $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{crit}}$, the curvature-matching
condition $\mathcal{R}\circ\iota=\mathcal{H}_0[\mathcal{G}]$ fails for at
least one stratum.  
By the Morphogenesis Theorem (Chapter~33), EHR rewrites are triggered,
altering the Polyxan skeleton and thereby crossing the boundary of the
current phase region.  
Conversely, if curvature stays below $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{crit}}$, all
type-curvature constraints remain solvable and no rewrite is forced,
so the system remains within a single smooth region.  
Thus equality determines the transition.
\end{proof}

\section{Historical Dynamics as RG Flow}

We now reinterpret historiography as geometric relaxation.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Long equilibria}  
      correspond to intervals where $\nabla\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{collective}}$
      is small; harmonic plateaus.

\item \textbf{Ideological bifurcations}  
      arise when the Polyxan skeleton admits two non-equivalent
      quine-stable extensions; these are saddle points in the epistemic
      potential.

\item \textbf{Renaissances}  
      occur when a long-standing tag entropy mismatch is suddenly resolved by
      a curvature-deficit drop, triggering EHR reduction and a new harmonic
      embedding.

\item \textbf{Civilizational collapse}  
      occurs when curvature exceeds $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{crit}}$ across a
      sufficiently large region, forcing simultaneous rewrites and radical
      contraction of $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{collective}}$.

\item \textbf{Global unification}  
      corresponds to quine-stable factorization: distinct belief strata
      become equivalent under the universal patch algebra, eliminating
      epistemic torsion.
\end{itemize}

These behaviours are not speculative analogies but directly follow from the
dynamical structure of the epistemic RG operator.

\section{The Civilizational Universality Theorem}

We now show that the epistemic RG flow possesses a universal intermediate
state, independent of the starting configuration.

\begin{theorem}[Civilizational Universality Theorem]
Let $X_0$ be any sufficiently complex civilizational configuration.
Then there exists a universal fixed profile $X_\ast$ such that
\[
\lim_{n\to n_{\mathrm{crit}}}
\mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}}^{\,n}(X_0)
=
X_\ast,
\]
and $X_\ast$ is independent of $X_0$ except for trivial symmetries.
This $X_\ast$ is the civilizational analogue of the universal critical
profile of Chapter~\ref{ch:36}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Contractivity ensures existence and uniqueness of fixed points for the
linearisation of $\mathsf{RG}_{\mathrm{ep}}$ near any stable configuration.  
Sufficiently complex initial data (those not already quine-stable or
near-harmonic) inevitably enter a high-curvature regime, where the critical
profile appears as the simplest nontrivial harmonic solution to the
rescaled field--hypergraph system.  
This fixed profile is stable by the spectral-gap argument of
Chapter~36.  
Uniqueness up to symmetry follows from the invariance of the RG operator
under the automorphism group of $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{collective}}$.
\end{proof}

\section{Epistemic Irreversibility and Historical Finality}

We now define the total epistemic central charge:
\[
c_{\mathrm{ep}}(t)
=
c_{\mathrm{RSVP}}(\Phi(t),\mathbf{v}(t),S(t))
+
c_{\mathrm{Polyx}}(\mathcal{G}(t)).
\]

\begin{theorem}[Epistemic Irreversibility]
For any civilizational trajectory under the epistemic RG flow,
\[
c_{\mathrm{ep}}(t+1)
<
c_{\mathrm{ep}}(t)
\quad
\text{unless the system is already at the quine-stable harmonic state}.
\]
Equality occurs if and only if the civilisation-wide configuration is the
flat embedding of the universal media quine, denoted
$\mathfrak{U}_{\mathrm{collective}}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Each component of the RG operator strictly decreases its own central charge:
harmonic projection decreases $c_{\mathrm{RSVP}}$, and EHR rewriting strictly decreases $c_{\mathrm{Polyx}}$ unless already at the unique normal form.
Because the sum of strictly decreasing Lyapunov functionals is strictly decreasing, the total central charge drops.  
Equality forces both components to be stationary, which by Part~III and Part~II can only happen at the universal quine.  
\end{proof}

\section{Final Cadence}

Under epistemic renormalization, a civilization is revealed as a transient
structure: a stratified, high-curvature perturbation of a single,
universal, quine-stable harmonic state.

\begin{quote}
A civilization is a finite-time renormalization event in the geometry  
of verified meaning.
\end{quote}
