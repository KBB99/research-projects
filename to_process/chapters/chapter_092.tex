\section{Definition of a Persona Cluster}

Let $\{\mathfrak{q}_1,\dots,\mathfrak{q}_n\}$ be a finite set of personas.

\begin{definition}[Distributed Persona Cluster]
A \emph{persona cluster} is the quotient
\[
\mathcal{C}
:= 
\mathrm{Hull}_{\mathrm{geo}}
\!\left(
\bigcup_{i=1}^n \Sigma_{\mathfrak{q}_i}
\right)
\bigg/
\!\sim_{\mathrm{cons}},
\]
where:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $\mathrm{Hull}_{\mathrm{geo}}$ is the geodesic-convex hull in the RSVP metric,
    \item $\sim_{\mathrm{cons}}$ is the constitutional equivalence relation generated by:
    \[
    a \sim_{\mathrm{cons}} b
    \iff
    \text{$a$ and $b$ are harmonically reconciled under the patch algebra},
    \]
    i.e.\ they represent the same stabilised meaning.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

Thus a persona cluster is the smallest geometric region in which all participating 
personas agree on stabilised content.

\section{Shared Agency as a Vector Field}

Agency is encoded in the RSVP vector field $v$.
For a cluster, we define:

\begin{definition}[Cluster Agency Field]
\[
v_{\mathrm{shared}}
:= 
\mathrm{Proj}_{\mathrm{harm}}
\left(
\sum_{i=1}^n w_i v_{\mathfrak{q}_i}
\right),
\]
where $w_i$ are constitutional weights satisfying $\sum_i w_i = 1$.
\end{definition}

The projection ensures:

\[
\nabla \cdot v_{\mathrm{shared}} = 0,
\]
i.e.\ shared agency is divergence-free:
no persona can unilaterally increase global tension in the cluster.
