\section{The Entropy--Curvature Feedback Operator}
\label{sec:feedback-operator}

Let $\mathcal{G}$ be a Polyxan hypergraph embedded by $\iota : \mathrm{Inc}(\mathcal{G}) \hookrightarrow \mathcal{M}$.  
Recall the two matching conditions of Chapter~31:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{R}\circ\iota &= \mathcal{H}_0[\mathcal{G}], \label{eq:curvature-matching}\\
S\circ\iota &= \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{tag}}[\mathcal{G}]. \label{eq:entropy-matching}
\end{align}
These imply that continuous curvature \emph{encodes} discrete curvature, and continuous entropy \emph{encodes} discrete tag entropy.

Define the \emph{closed-loop feedback operator} $\mathcal{F}$ on pairs $(\mathcal{G},\iota)$ by
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{F}[\mathcal{G},\iota]
  = \Bigl(
      \mathcal{H}_0[\mathcal{G}] - \mathcal{R}\circ\iota,\;
      \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{tag}}[\mathcal{G}] - S\circ\iota
    \Bigr).
\end{equation}
By the coupled dynamics, the L²-gradient of $\mathcal{F}$ drives:
\begin{itemize}
\item increases in discrete curvature to source increases in continuous entropy,  
\item increases in continuous curvature to source discrete curvature hoisting or reduction,  
\item tag mismatch to drive vertex migration,  
\item curvature mismatch to drive hyperedge stretching or snapping.
\end{itemize}

Thus entropy and curvature do not just match: they \emph{react back on one another} through the embedding flow and the EHR rewriting system.  
This feedback is the engine of multi-galaxy cosmology.

\section{Closed-Loop Evolution and Mutual Back-Reaction}
\label{sec:closed-loop-evolution}

We now compute the explicit evolution.

\subsection{Discrete-to-continuous feedback}
Define the instantaneous feedback contributions:
\begin{align}
\partial_t S
  &\supset + \gamma\,(\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{tag}}[\mathcal{G}] - S\circ\iota)\,\delta_{\iota(\mathcal{G})}, \\
\partial_t \mathcal{R}
  &\supset + \beta\,(\mathcal{H}_0[\mathcal{G}] - \mathcal{R}\circ\iota)\,\delta_{\iota(\mathcal{G})}.
\end{align}
Thus discrete curvature deficits produce entropic spikes on $\mathcal{M}$, while discrete tag mismatches produce curvature spikes.

\subsection{Continuous-to-discrete feedback}

Conversely, when the embedding violates curvature or entropy matching, the discrete system responds:
\begin{align}
\partial_t \mathcal{H}_0[\mathcal{G}]
  &\supset -\beta\,\nabla (\mathcal{R}\circ\iota)\cdot \nabla \iota, \\
\partial_t \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{tag}}[\mathcal{G}]
  &\supset -\gamma\,\nabla S\cdot\nabla \iota.
\end{align}
Hyperedges shear and vertices migrate until equilibrium is restored.

\subsection{Closed feedback loop}

Combining both directions yields the closed-loop system:
\[
\partial_t
\begin{pmatrix}
\mathcal{R} \\
S \\[2pt]
\mathcal{H}_0[\mathcal{G}] \\
\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{tag}}[\mathcal{G}]
\end{pmatrix}
=
\mathcal{L}
\begin{pmatrix}
\mathcal{R} - \mathcal{H}_0[\mathcal{G}] \\
S - \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{tag}}[\mathcal{G}]
\end{pmatrix}
 + \text{(migration + EHR corrections)},
\]
where $\mathcal{L}$ is a strictly dissipative operator.  
Thus entropy and curvature jointly relax toward matching in finite time.

\section{Semantic Galaxy Clusters}
\label{sec:galaxy-clusters}

A \emph{semantic galaxy} $\mathfrak{G}$ is a quine-stable hypergraph embedded harmonically in an elliptic RSVP basin.  
A \emph{semantic galaxy cluster} is a finite collection
\[
\mathfrak{G}_1,\dots,\mathfrak{G}_N
\]
of such galaxies connected pairwise (possibly sparsely) by \emph{curvature--entropy tubes}:
\[
\mathcal{T}_{ij} \subset \mathcal{M}
\quad (i\ne j).
\]
These tubes are regions where curvature mismatch and entropy mismatch remain non-zero because the galaxies are not yet fully isolated.

\subsection{Energy of a tube}

The energy of a tube is
\begin{equation}
E_{\mathrm{tube}}[\mathcal{T}_{ij}]
  = \int_{\mathcal{T}_{ij}}
    \bigl(

      +\gamma(S-\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{tag}}[\mathcal{T}])^2
    \bigr)dV_g.
\end{equation}
Negative tube energy indicates an attractive configuration.

\section{The N-Galaxy Virial Theorem}
\label{sec:n-galaxy-virial}

Consider $N$ galaxies with positions $x_i=\mathrm{barycenter}(\mathfrak{G}_i)$ and velocities induced by the embedding flow:
\[
u_i = \partial_t x_i.
\]
Define the total moment of inertia 
\[
I = \sum_i m_i \lVert x_i\rVert^2,
\qquad m_i = \mathrm{mass}(\mathfrak{G}_i) := \int_{\iota(\mathfrak{G}_i)} S\,dV.
\]
The second derivative satisfies
\[
\frac12 \frac{d^2 I}{dt^2}
  = 2K + \sum_{i<j} E_{\mathrm{bind}}[\mathcal{T}_{ij}],
\]
where $K$ is the kinetic energy of galaxy migration.

\begin{theorem}[N-Galaxy Virial Theorem]
No galaxy cluster with $N\ge2$ can satisfy a steady-state virial balance unless all binding energies vanish.  
In that case the system is unbound and disperses.  
Thus no eternally virialised cluster exists.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A virialised state requires
\[
2K + \sum_{i<j}E_{\mathrm{bind}} = 0.
\]
But $E_{\mathrm{bind}}<0$ implies collapse (Galaxy Binding Theorem).  
$E_{\mathrm{bind}}>0$ implies dissolution.  
The only way to maintain equality is for all $E_{\mathrm{bind}}=0$, which removes all interactions.  
Thus no stable virialised cluster exists.
\end{proof}
