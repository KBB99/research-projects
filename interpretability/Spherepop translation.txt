Instead of starting from sets, states, or timeless axioms, it begins with events that happen only once, change what is possible afterward, and leave an auditable history. 

Structure arises from a constructive, history-based mereology, where part-whole relations are formed over time. Identity is not prior but historical: two things are the same if they share the same event history. By avoiding global membership and unrestricted comprehension, it sidesteps classical paradoxes and ensures that complexity grows only with what actually happens.

As a visual language, it uses nested scopes represented as "bubbles" that are evaluated explicitly, making scope, order, and dependency visible. Spherepop thus proposes a more faithful foundation for modeling real systems—cognitive, computational, and social—defined by their irreversible history rather than abstract states.
