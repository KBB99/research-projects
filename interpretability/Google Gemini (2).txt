Scope Memeplexes

In "Scope Memeplexes: The Enshittification Crisis as an Interface War," Flyxion argues that the degradation of digital platforms—commonly called "enshittification"—is not primarily caused by corporate greed or managerial failure, but is an
emergent evolutionary phenomenon
.
The paper models interfaces as "macroscopic artificial intellects" or
scope memeplexes
that compete for survival by colonizing human attentional and motor bandwidth
.
Main Arguments
Interfaces as Organisms
: Platforms are viewed as informational organisms that grow, mutate, and compete within a cognitive ecosystem
.
Their primary resource is human sensorimotor bandwidth rather than capital
.
The Theory of Scope Memeplexes
: A scope memeplex is a self-propagating interface pattern that restructures which actions are cognitively easy or difficult
.
They compete through "embodiment," reshaping user behavior and the very "topology of agency"
.
Interface Phonemes and Shibboleths
: Every interaction grammar (e.g., clicks, swipes, or Vim commands) establishes a set of minimal operational distinctions termed "interface phonemes"
.
These distinctions function as "shibboleths," separating fluent users from novices through embodied competence rather than propositional knowledge
.
The "Parasitic" Mouse
: The paper characterizes the mouse-based interface as a cognitive parasite
.
It replaces symbolic, compositional thought (typical of keyboard-centric interaction) with continuous spatial targeting, which fragments skill and keeps users in a state of perpetual novicehood
.
Monopolar Foraging
: Modern platforms like TikTok or Instagram reduce user action to a single repetitive gesture (the upward swipe), which the paper describes as a "contraction of human motor ecology"
.
This "monopolar" grammar returns users to a primitive foraging strategy—scan, approach, consume, repeat—optimized for extraction rather than construction
.
Conclusions
Failure of Ecological Governance
: Enshittification is reframed as a "failure of ecological governance in an emerging cognitive biosphere"
.
It is the result of unchecked selection pressure that favors
transmissibility
(ease of adoption) over
expressivity
(depth of agency)
.
Linguistic Monoculture
: The crisis is a "linguistic monoculture" where symbolic diversity collapses because interface organisms optimize for short-term capture
.
The Home Row as Resistance
: Defending high-dimensional, keyboard-centric interaction (like the "home row") is viewed as an evolutionary stance to preserve human-machine symbiosis against "perceptual capture systems"
.
Need for Intervention
: The paper concludes that the path forward requires "ecological intervention" to protect symbiotic interface lineages that amplify human agency instead of consuming it
.

"Scope Memeplexes: The Enshittification Crisis as an Interface War" by Flyxion (2026) provides a radical evolutionary reinterpretation of platform degradation
.
Rather than blaming corporate greed, it models interfaces as informational organisms that compete for survival by colonizing human attention and motor bandwidth
.
1. Core Theory: The Scope Memeplex
The paper introduces the
scope memeplex
, defined as a self-propagating interface pattern that restructures which human actions are cognitively easy or unthinkable
.
Artilects
: Interfaces function as macroscopic artificial intellects ("artilects")
.
Ecological Competition
: Platforms compete in a cognitive ecosystem where their "success" is measured by their ability to "capture" user behavior
.
The Evolutionary Lens
: Enshittification is reframed as
runaway selection
, where interface organisms optimize for their own replication (transmissibility) rather than user agency or semantic fidelity (expressivity)
.
2. Theoretical Breakdown
The paper builds its argument through several key concepts:
A. Interface Phonemes and the Shibboleth Effect
Operational Phonemes
: Minimal action units like keystrokes, clicks, or swipes are "interface phonemes"
.
The Shibboleth Effect
: Mastery of complex interface grammars (e.g., Vim) creates an "embodied competence" that separates fluent users from novices
.
Interface Accent
: A user's "accent" is visible through their reliance on menus versus shortcuts
.
B. The Mouse as a "Cognitive Parasite"
Symbolic vs. Perceptual
: Keyboard-based interaction is
symbolic and compositional
(like language), allowing for complex, abstract thought
.
Spatial Targeting
: The mouse displaces this with
continuous spatial targeting
, forcing the brain to constantly reorient itself
.
Fragmentation
: Mouse-based skills are application-specific and do not scale, keeping users in a state of "perpetual novicehood" that benefits the platform
.
C. Monopolar Foraging
Primitive Strategy
: The infinite vertical swipe is compared to the "primitive foraging strategy" of grazing animals: scan, approach, consume, repeat
.
Devolution
: This "monopolar" grammar collapses high-dimensional human motor ecology (which is naturally parallel and complex) into a single repetitive reflex
.
3. Analysis of "Enshittification"
The paper argues that enshittification is a "linguistic monoculture" resulting from the collapse of symbolic diversity
.
Transmissibility over Expressivity
: Selection pressure favors interfaces that are easy to adopt but hard to master, leading to a "pidginization" of interaction where global expressive capacity collapses
.
Institutional Failure as Evolutionary Overshoot
: Systems that seem to act against their long-term interests are actually acting rationally as "replicators" optimizing for short-term propagation
.
